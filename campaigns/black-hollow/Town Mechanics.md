# **Black Hollow Rebuilding Ruleset**  

*(A structured system for restoring, managing, and shaping the future of Black Hollow using Bastion-style mechanics.)*  

---

## **1. Core Mechanics: Settlement Management**  

Black Hollow operates as a **community Bastion** that multiple characters can contribute to and benefit from. The settlement begins in a state of collapse and can be rebuilt using familiar Bastion mechanics.

### **A. Settlement Stability & Facility Integration**

Black Hollow's overall health is determined by the **number and quality of functioning facilities** rather than an arbitrary scale. Each facility contributes to different aspects of town life:

- **Security**: Guildhall, Staging Post, Militia facilities
- **Economy**: Supply Shop, Forge, Trade facilities  
- **Morale**: Tavern, Church, Cultural facilities
- **Growth**: Available housing and infrastructure

**Settlement Benefits Scale with Facility Development:**

- **1-2 Functional Facilities**: Basic services, high prices, frequent threats
- **3-4 Functional Facilities**: Stable services, normal prices, occasional threats  
- **5-6 Functional Facilities**: Growing economy, below-market prices, rare threats
- **7+ Functional Facilities**: Thriving hub, excellent prices, strong defenses

---

## **2. Facility Development System**

Each major building in Black Hollow functions as a **Special Facility** that can be developed through the standard Bastion system. Characters can contribute to town development during **Bastion turns**.

### **Facility Space & Development Costs**

All town facilities follow the standard Bastion space and cost structure:

| **Development Stage** | **Space** | **Cost** | **Time Required** | **Description** |
|---------------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Ruined** | N/A | N/A | N/A | **Non-functional, requires rebuilding** |
| **Basic** | Cramped | 500 GP | 20 days | **Minimal services, limited capacity** |
| **Functional** | Roomy | +500 GP | +25 days | **Full services, normal capacity** |
| **Thriving** | Vast | +2,000 GP | +80 days | **Premium services, expanded capacity** |

**Development Method**: Characters use the **Craft order** during Bastion turns to contribute to facility development. Multiple characters can contribute to the same project, pooling resources and time.

## **4. Community Bastion Management**

### **A. Shared Bastion Turns**

Black Hollow operates on the same **7-day Bastion turn** schedule as character Bastions. During each turn:

1. **Any character in town** can issue orders to town facilities
2. **Multiple characters** can coordinate on the same projects
3. **Absentee contributions** can be made through Sending or similar magic
4. **Maintain orders** trigger Settlement Events (see below)

### **B. Resource Pooling**

Characters can **pool resources** for town development:

- **Gold contributions** can be combined for facility upgrades
- **Time contributions** through Craft orders can accelerate development
- **Spell assistance** can reduce costs or time (DM discretion)

### **C. Shared Benefits**

All contributors gain access to facility benefits:

- **Crafting services** available to all allies
- **Information networks** benefit the entire party
- **Defensive benefits** protect all residents and allies
- **Trade advantages** apply to all who use the facilities

## **5. Settlement Population System**

Black Hollow's population grows organically based on **facility development** and **available housing**. The system uses historical medieval demographics to ensure realistic population distribution and growth patterns.

### **A. Population Categories & Demographics**

Based on medieval settlement patterns, populations are divided into social and economic classes:

**Settlement Officers** (~1.5% of population):

- **Reeve**: Chief administrator and tax collector
- **Constable**: Law enforcement leader 
- **Woodward**: Forest and land warden
- **Law Enforcement**: Guards, watchmen (1 per 150 people)

**Clergy** (~0.5% of population):

- **Clerics**: Lesser clergy, scribes, acolytes (1 per 120 people)
- **Priests**: Full clergy conducting services (1 per 30 clerics)

**Freeholders** (~6.5% of population):

- **Skilled Craftspeople**: Following medieval guild frequencies
- **Independent Farmers**: Those who own their land
- **Merchants**: Those engaged in trade

**Citizens** (~91.5% of population):

- **Unfree Tenants**: Work lord's land in exchange for housing
- **Laborers**: Unskilled workers and servants
- **Household Members**: Families of all above classes

### **B. Medieval Population Growth Mechanics**

**Settlement Size Classifications:**

- **Hamlet**: 10-100 people
- **Village**: 101-400 people  
- **Large Village**: 401-900 people
- **Town**: 901-3,000 people
- **Large Town**: 3,001-8,000 people
- **City**: 8,001-20,000 people

**Population Growth Drivers:**
Growth is **not automatic** but driven by **economic opportunity** and **safety**:

| **Economic Factors** | **Population Draw** | **Requirements** |
|---------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Basic Services** | +10% growth potential | Functional Tavern, Shop, Church |
| **Craft Industries** | +15% growth potential | Functional Smithy, Workshop, specialized facilities |
| **Trade Hub** | +20% growth potential | Functional Storehouse, Staging Post, good roads |
| **Regional Center** | +25% growth potential | Thriving Town Hall, multiple Thriving facilities |

**Safety & Stability Factors:**

- **Secure Roads**: +5% growth bonus (Staging Post operational)
- **Local Defense**: +5% growth bonus (Militia/Barrack facilities)
- **Supernatural Safety**: +10% growth bonus (major threats eliminated)
- **Ongoing Threats**: -10% to -25% growth penalty

### **C. Realistic Housing Requirements**

**Medieval Household Sizes:**

- **Noble Households**: 8-12 people (family, servants, guards)
- **Freeholder Families**: 4.0 people average
- **Citizen Households**: 4.75 people average

**Housing Development** follows standard Bastion costs:

**Residential Quarter** (New Facility Type):

| **Stage** | **Space** | **Households** | **Population Capacity** |
|----------|-----------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Basic** | Cramped | 6 households | ~25-30 people |
| **Functional** | Roomy | 12 households | ~50-60 people |
| **Thriving** | Vast | 24 households | ~100-120 people |

### **D. Economic Benefits (Medieval-Based)**

**Taxation & Trade Income:**

- **Hamlet** (10-100): 2 GP per week per 20 people
- **Village** (101-400): 3 GP per week per 20 people  
- **Large Village** (401-900): 4 GP per week per 20 people
- **Town** (901+): 5 GP per week per 20 people

**Freeholder Benefits:**
Each functional craft facility attracts 1-2 freeholders of relevant type:

- **Increased tax base**: Freeholders pay higher taxes
- **Specialized services**: Reduce facility upgrade costs by 10% when relevant freeholders present
- **Guild formation**: 3+ freeholders of same type can form guild (additional economic benefits)

## **5. Settlement Events System**

Instead of arbitrary town events, Black Hollow uses a modified **Bastion Events** system when anyone issues a **Maintain order** to the settlement.

**Settlement Events Table** (Roll 1d100):

| **Roll** | **Event** | **Effect** |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **01-40** | **All Is Well** | **Minor positive developments, no mechanical effect** |
| **41-50** | **Trade Opportunity** | **Storehouse gains 1d6 × 50 GP worth of goods** |
| **51-55** | **Bandit Threat** | **Security facilities can issue Recruit orders at half cost this turn** |
| **56-60** | **Traveler's Tale** | **Information-gathering facilities gain bonus Research die** |
| **61-65** | **Merchant Caravan** | **Trade facilities can issue orders at reduced cost** |
| **66-70** | **Skilled Immigrant** | **One facility gains temporary expert hireling (advantage on next order)** |
| **71-75** | **Resource Discovery** | **Craft facilities can complete one minor project at no cost** |
| **76-80** | **Pilgrim Donation** | **Church facilities gain 1d6 × 25 GP donation** |
| **81-85** | **Faction Interest** | **Roll for which faction offers assistance or makes demands** |
| **86-90** | **Infrastructure Need** | **Opportunity to upgrade at reduced cost with community effort** |
| **91-95** | **Celebration** | **Morale boost - all facilities operate at +1 efficiency next turn** |
| **96-100** | **Crisis** | **Major threat requires coordinated response from multiple facilities** |

## **7. End Goals & Victory Conditions**

Success is measured by **facility development milestones** rather than arbitrary thresholds:

✔ **Frontier Outpost** (3+ Functional facilities): Basic services restored, regular trade
✔ **Growing Town** (5+ Functional facilities): Expanding population, diverse economy  
✔ **Regional Hub** (7+ Functional facilities): Strategic importance, political influence
✔ **Thriving City** (All facilities Thriving): Major population center, cultural significance

**Ultimate Fate Determined by Player Choices:**

- **Facility focus** determines the town's character and specialization
- **Faction alliances** shape political and economic structures  
- **Resource allocation** affects growth patterns and sustainability
- **Community engagement** influences long-term stability and prosperity
