# The Nemesis: The Gravedigger

## **The Double Game**

The Gravedigger operates on multiple levels of deception:

**Public Face:**

- Respected academic researcher operating as **Magistra (Emerita) Morwyn Ashfeld**
- Expert in ancient human civilizations
- Seemingly supportive of local authorities
- Professional relationship with other scholars
- **Manipulation through scholarship**: Uses carefully crafted archaeological reports to manipulate targets into desired actions (such as convincing Lord Blackthorne to excavate the manor)

**Cabal Cover:**

- Researching "human origins" to support Cabal's goals
- Appears to seek evidence of humanity's divine-free destiny
- Presents findings that support human supremacy
- Maintains appearance of loyalty to Cabal leadership

**True Agenda:**

- Personal quest for immortality
- Communication with the Aboleth
- Uses Cabal resources while hiding true purpose
- Must avoid both public and Cabal scrutiny

## **Goals and Motivations**

**Primary Goal:** Achieve true immortality through the Aboleth's knowledge

- Not just extended life, but transcendence beyond even draconic limitations
- Seeks to understand the fundamental nature of existence
- Wants to surpass the limitations of both mortality and divine power

**Cabal-Facing Goals:**

1. Research ancient human civilizations
2. Find evidence of human power independent of gods
3. Support the Cabal's human supremacy agenda
4. Maintain position within Cabal hierarchy

**True Secondary Goals:**

1. Maintain both academic and Cabal covers
2. Expand her network of cultists and informants
3. Collect and preserve ancient knowledge
4. Create a power base in Black Hollow

**Personal Drives:**

- Fear of death and limitation
- Intellectual curiosity taken to extreme
- Pride in her methodical approach
- Desire to prove dragons can transcend their nature
- Resentment of the Green Dragon's manipulation

## **Methods and Resources**

**Primary Tools:**

- Network of cultists performing controlled experiments
- Academic credentials and connections
- Centuries of accumulated knowledge and artifacts
- Her own draconic powers (used sparingly)
- Cabal resources (carefully misappropriated)

**Key Resources:**

- The Cult of Vaelthas (unwitting servants)
- Her position in the academic community
- Hidden caches of forbidden knowledge
- Ancient artifacts and ritual items
- Cabal funding and protection

**Operational Style:**

- Works through intermediaries
- Uses multiple parallel approaches
- Maintains careful documentation
- Prefers corruption to destruction
- Compartmentalizes different aspects of operation

## **The Cabal Dynamic**

**Relationship with Cabal Leadership:**

- Appears loyal to the Green Dragon's vision
- Provides regular reports on "human origins" research
- Maintains careful distance from other dragon members
- Uses Cabal politics to deflect scrutiny

**Cover Operation:**

- Legitimate archaeological research
- Documentation of ancient human achievements
- Study of pre-divine human civilizations
- Collection of artifacts supporting human potential

**Resource Management:**

- Diverts Cabal resources to personal goals
- Maintains two sets of research records
- Uses cultists to separate Cabal work from true aims
- Creates plausible connections between official and secret work

## **Weaknesses and Flaws**

**Character Flaws:**

- Overconfidence in her ability to maintain multiple deceptions
- Inability to trust or delegate important tasks
- Obsession with perfection in her experiments
- Tendency to overcomplicate plans
- Underestimation of the Green Dragon's awareness

**Exploitable Weaknesses:**

- Must maintain both human and Cabal disguises
- Cannot risk exposure to academic or Cabal community
- Protective of her research and records
- Unwilling to abandon long-term plans
- Vulnerable to exposure from multiple directions

## **Development Through Campaign**

**Early Game (Levels 1-4): Shadows and Whispers**

- No direct appearances
- Her presence felt only through consequences:
  - Strange archaeological digs around town
  - Missing research materials from the library
  - Cultists carrying coded research notes
  - Scholars speaking of a respected but never-seen academic
- Clues left in:
  - Recovered documents
  - Cultist correspondence
  - Local rumors
  - Archaeological site patterns

**Mid Game (Levels 5-7): Growing Influence**

- First indirect interactions through intermediaries
- Escalation of cultist activities
- Her research begins affecting the town more visibly
- Players might glimpse her from afar or in disguise
- Signs of her influence in:
  - Cabal operations becoming more frequent
  - Increased archaeological activity
  - More dangerous experiments
  - Scholarly debates about human origins

**Late Game (Levels 8-10): The Revelation**

- True nature gradually revealed
- Direct confrontation becomes possible
- Full scope of plans uncovered
- Final push for immortality
- Players understand:
  - Her manipulation of both Cabal and cultists
  - The true purpose of the archaeological research
  - Her connection to the Aboleth
  - The extent of her deception

## **Early Game Presentation**

**How Players Learn of Her:**

1. **Initial Contact:**
   - Black Sword Company ambush
   - Professional mercenary tactics
   - Organized regional presence
   - Connection to local events unclear initially

2. **Archaeological Sites:**
   - Unusual dig patterns
   - Strangely specific research focuses
   - Artifacts gone missing

3. **Cultist Activities:**
   - References to "The Scholar" in coded messages
   - Specific instructions about research and artifacts
   - Careful documentation requirements
   - Use of Prophet's gift to locate ancient artifacts
   - Failed attempt to secure the Ithilnorith

4. **Local Impact:**
   - Scholars excited about new historical theories
   - Missing books from specific topics
   - Funding appearing for certain projects
   - Strange lights in abandoned buildings at night

5. **Documentary Evidence:**
   - Partial research notes
   - Funding request papers
   - Correspondence with other academics
   - Supply requisition forms
   - Prophetic visions transcripts regarding artifacts

**The Prophet's Crypt Operation:**

The Gravedigger's first major setback came from the failed attempt to secure the Ithilnorith:

- Used the Prophet's gift to locate the sealed tome
- Established cult cell in Black Hollow to retrieve it
- Underestimated local resistance and Gilded Dawn presence
- Lost both the book and the cult cell
- Must now adapt plans while maintaining distance

**Key Artifacts in Play:**

1. **The Ithilnorith (The Thirsting Codex)**
   - Currently sealed, appearing as moon cult writings
   - Contains potential knowledge about immortality
   - May hold clues about the Aboleth's prison
   - Critical to the Gravedigger's research

2. **The Prophet's Gift**
   - Used to locate powerful artifacts
   - Helps identify potential ritual sites
   - Guides excavation efforts
   - Limited by user's understanding
   - Source: The imprisoned Prophet in the manor

**The Prophet and the Manor:**

The Gravedigger's current focus centers on the manor's excavation:

- The Prophet (a crazed madre) lies imprisoned in a Dragon Cult sarcophagus
- Their prophetic abilities are key to locating artifacts of power
- The manor excavation serves multiple purposes:
  - Maintaining access to the Prophet
  - Searching for additional artifacts
  - Establishing a permanent base of operations
  - Protecting the source of prophetic guidance

**The Manor's Dark History:**

The location's significance stems from its former owner:

- The old lord was a Cabal member
- Discovered evidence of the Aboleth's presence
- Attempted to form private alliance with the Gravedigger
- Made fatal error of trying to share power
- His fate serves as example of Gravedigger's ruthlessness
- Manor chosen as base specifically to control this legacy

**The Black Sword Company Connection:**

The Gravedigger maintains regional control through mercenary forces:

- Black Sword Company serves as primary enforcers
- Responsible for the manor raid that "eliminated" the old lord
- Possible transformation of lord into mindless entity
- Currently patrol region and harass travelers
- Attempted ambush of Gilded Dawn agents
- Maintain deniable connection to excavation activities

## Current Operations

### The Cabal's Current Focus
The Gravedigger's current focus centers on The Mines:

1. **Archaeological Expertise**: Using her legitimate credentials to guide systematic exploration
2. **Undead Labor**: Employing intelligent undead servants for dangerous excavation work
3. **Knowledge Extraction**: Seeking specific artifacts and information from the ancient ruins

The manor's The Mines serves multiple purposes:
- **Cover Operation**: Provides legitimate archaeological explanation for activities
- **Resource Extraction**: Recovering ancient artifacts and knowledge
- **Strategic Position**: Establishes base of operations in the region
- **Testing Ground**: Allows experimentation with new undead creation methods

### Network Management
- **Regional Coordination**: Managing cult cells across multiple communities
- **Intelligence Gathering**: Using undead agents to monitor threats and opportunities
- **Resource Allocation**: Directing cult resources to support her primary goals
- **Maintain deniable connection to The Mines activities**

### Long-term Strategic Goals
1. **The Mines:**
   - Secure access to the ancient ruins
   - Extract maximum knowledge and artifacts
   - Establish permanent base of operations
   - Use discoveries to enhance her power

2. **Regional Domination:**
   - Expand cult influence across the region
   - Replace key officials with undead servants
   - Control trade routes and information flow
   - Establish herself as the hidden ruler

3. **Undead Perfection:**
   - Create more sophisticated undead servants
   - Develop methods for mass undead creation
   - Achieve personal immortality through undeath
   - Build an army of intelligent undead

4. **Divine Transcendence:**
   - Accumulate enough power to challenge the gods
   - Establish herself as a new deity of death
   - Create a new world order with undead as the ruling class
   - Achieve ultimate revenge against those who wronged her

## Campaign Integration

### Phase 1: The Surface Mystery (Levels 1-3)
- **Introduction**: The PCs encounter cult activities through investigating local mysteries
- **Escalation**: They discover evidence of a larger network and organized threat
- **Revelation**: The Gravedigger's identity and connection to the manor are revealed
- **Climax**: Direct confrontation with major cult operations

### Phase 2: The Network War (Levels 4-6)
- **Expansion**: The threat spreads to multiple communities and regions
- **Allies**: The PCs build coalitions to resist the cult's influence
- **Strikes**: Major operations against cult strongholds and leadership
- **Revelation**: The true scope of The Gravedigger's plan becomes clear

### Phase 3: The Final Confrontation (Levels 7-10)
- **Escalation**: The Gravedigger abandons subtlety for direct action
- **Stakes**: The fate of the entire region hangs in the balance
- **Allies**: All accumulated allies join the final battle
- **Resolution**: Ultimate confrontation with The Gravedigger herself

## Tactical Considerations

### The Gravedigger's Advantages
- **Intelligence Network**: Undead agents provide comprehensive surveillance
- **Legitimate Cover**: Academic credentials allow access to restricted areas
- **Resource Control**: Cult provides funding, manpower, and materials
- **Undead Servants**: Loyal agents who cannot be corrupted or compromised

### The Gravedigger's Vulnerabilities
- **Overconfidence**: Believes her plans are too sophisticated to be discovered
- **Emotional Triggers**: Personal history with certain locations and people
- **Dependence**: Relies heavily on her undead network for information and execution
- **Isolation**: Trusts no one fully, making her vulnerable to betrayal

### For the DM
- **Escalation**: Each encounter should reveal more about the network's scope
- **Personal Stakes**: Connect the threat to the PCs' personal stories
- **Moral Complexity**: Some cult members are victims rather than villains
- **Long-term Consequences**: The PCs' actions shape the campaign's ultimate outcome

The Gravedigger represents the **ultimate culmination** of the campaign's themes: the corruption of knowledge, the price of ambition, and the question of whether revenge can ever truly be justified. Her defeat should feel earned through the PCs' growth and the alliances they've built throughout the campaign.

## **Physical and Emotional Characteristics**

**Academic Persona:**

- Tall, elegant woman in her apparent mid-40s
- Sharp, angular features that command attention
- Steel-gray hair always impeccably styled in a practical bun
- Favors well-tailored, dark academic robes with subtle draconic patterns
- Carries herself with precise, measured movements
- Voice is clear and resonant, perfect for lectures
- Always wears a distinctive silver pendant of intertwined serpents
- Hands bear subtle ink stains from constant note-taking

**Behavioral Patterns:**

- Speaks with careful consideration, choosing each word deliberately
- Maintains unwavering eye contact during conversations
- Has a habit of adjusting her pendant when deep in thought
- Takes meticulous notes during any discussion
- Occasionally lapses into academic jargon when excited about a topic
- Demonstrates remarkable patience in explanations
- Shows genuine enthusiasm when discussing ancient history

**Emotional Tells:**

- Slight tightening around eyes when information surprises her
- Drumming fingers on nearest surface when plans go awry
- Brief pause before responding to unexpected questions
- Subtle shift in posture when someone mentions immortality
- Tendency to straighten already-perfect clothing when nervous
- Momentary flash of draconic anger in eyes when truly challenged

**Distinctive Quirks:**

- Always arranges items on her desk in perfect geometric patterns
- Carries multiple journals, each with different colored bindings
- Never sits with her back to a door or window
- Has a collection of ancient coins she absently handles while thinking
- Speaks multiple languages but occasionally mixes archaic terms
- Maintains immaculate organization in all her spaces

**True Form Hints:**

- Shadow sometimes suggests larger form in certain lights
- Room temperature drops slightly in her presence
- Faint smell of old parchment and mineral dust follows her
- Occasional metallic gleam in eyes when angered
- Voice carries subtle resonance in enclosed spaces
- Movement has an underlying serpentine grace

**Professional Charisma:**

- Commands attention through expertise rather than charm
- Builds trust through demonstrated knowledge
- Shows genuine interest in others' research
- Offers measured praise for intellectual achievements
- Creates comfortable academic atmosphere in conversations
- Maintains professional boundaries while being approachable

**Stress Responses:**

- Becomes increasingly formal when threatened
- Speech patterns shift to more archaic forms under pressure
- Subtle scale-like patterns appear on neck when enraged
- Hands become unnaturally still when preparing to act
- Voice gains harmonics that affect listeners' concentration
- Temperature around her drops noticeably when control slips

**Social Interactions:**

- Prefers small groups or one-on-one meetings
- Excellent listener who remembers every detail
- Deflects personal questions with academic discussions
- Shows unexpected kindness to dedicated students
- Maintains network through academic correspondence
- Never makes promises she doesn't intend to keep

These characteristics serve to:

- Reinforce her academic cover
- Hint at her true nature
- Provide recognizable patterns across disguises
- Offer subtle warnings of her moods
- Create memorable interactions without relying on seduction
- Demonstrate her power through knowledge and presence

## **Memorable Hints & Phrases**

**Visual Signatures:**

- "Those perfect geometric patterns... only one scholar I know is that precise."
- "See how the ink never bleeds? Each letter contained, like a prisoner."
- "That silver pendant - two serpents eating knowledge. The old university seal."
- "Even her shadow seems too large for her frame."
- "Notice how she never lets anyone stand behind her desk?"

**Environmental Effects:**

- "The archives always feel colder when she's researching."
- "Funny how the candles dim when she enters a room."
- "Did you see how the ravens went silent as she passed?"
- "Even the dust settles in perfect circles around her books."
- "The air tastes of old parchment and stone when she speaks."

**Behavioral Patterns:**

- "Three journals - red for blood, black for secrets, white for lies."
- "She has this way of looking at you... like she's reading a particularly fascinating scroll."
- "Always asks three questions. Never the ones you expect."
- "Touches those ancient coins when she's plotting something."
- "Have you noticed? She never makes promises... just 'observations'."

**Academic Reputation:**

- "The Scholar? Her research is impeccable... too impeccable."
- "They say her private library holds books that shouldn't exist."
- "Every expedition she funds finds exactly what she predicted."
- "Her theories about human potential... they change people."
- "Strange how her critics tend to abandon their research."

**Cultist Whispers:**

- "The Enlightened One sees through many eyes."
- "Knowledge flows like water, but wisdom burns like dragon's breath."
- "She collects secrets like others collect coins."
- "The Scholar's mark - perfect circles, perfect squares, perfect traps."
- "Even the Prophet speaks carefully in her presence."

**Black Sword Company Rumors:**

- "Payment always exact, orders always precise."
- "Gold that smells of old books and tastes of dust."
- "Jobs for the Scholar? Clean hands, bloody minds."
- "Watch for the serpent seal - those jobs pay triple."
- "She knows before we fail. Always knows."

**Local Impact:**

- "The university's changed since she started funding research."
- "Books go missing, but knowledge seems to multiply."
- "Young scholars enter her lectures eager, leave... different."
- "They say she can read a lifetime's research in an afternoon."
- "Strange lights in the old archives, always on the moonless nights."

**Prophetic Fragments:**

- "The serpent that devours knowledge grows ever longer..."
- "In perfect geometries, the truth lies bound..."
- "She walks between shadow and form, neither fully either..."
- "The dragon that reads seeks not gold, but time itself..."
- "When wisdom bleeds, she will be there to collect it..."

These hints serve multiple purposes:

- Create consistent themes across different contexts
- Provide recognizable patterns without direct confirmation
- Allow for gradual player realization
- Offer multiple interpretation layers
- Connect various aspects of her operation
- Plant seeds for future revelations

Usage Guidelines:

1. Introduce hints gradually and naturally
2. Vary sources and contexts
3. Allow some contradictions to maintain mystery
4. Use more frequently as her influence grows
5. Let players make connections themselves
6. Save some reveals for key moments
