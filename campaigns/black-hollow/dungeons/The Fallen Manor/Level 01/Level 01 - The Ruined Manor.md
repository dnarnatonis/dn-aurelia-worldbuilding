# The Fallen Manor of Lord Blackthorne - Level 01

## Overview - Expanded

The crumbling Blackthorne Manor stands as a monument to ambition and folly. Decades ago, Lord Matthias Blackthorne—driven by scholarly curiosity and forbidden knowledge—transformed his cellar into a necromantic laboratory. Working in secret collaboration with The Gravedigger's network, he perfected the art of creating revenants using the ancient convergence point beneath his manor. His final experiment, attempting to transform himself into an immortal undead lord, went catastrophically wrong, creating the mindless abomination now known as the Hollow Lord.

The corruption that now permeates the manor stems from multiple sources: the Hollow Lord's presence in the undercroft below, combined with Madre Eudokia's connection to the Aboleth influence from the convergence point. His insatiable hunger for souls and compulsive necromantic rituals, merged with the otherworldly taint of the ancient entity, have turned the entire structure into a nexus of undeath and aberrant influence, driving the Deep Goblins upward from even deeper caverns and creating the tense situation that exists today.

Today, the manor's first level is a contested battleground between two factions, each with their own motivations and secrets. The imposing stone walls that once hosted nobility now echo with the sounds of tribal warfare and desperate survival.

## Faction Dynamics

### The Deep Goblins

**Nature:** Religious zealots driven upward from deeper caverns by the spreading corruption below. They worship an entity they call "The Whispering Dark" and believe themselves chosen to spread its influence.

**Leadership:** Warlord Grikzash, a battle-scarred goblin who leads through strength and cunning. While he exploits the tribe's religious fervor, it is his advisor Zekuth, the Goblin Dark Priest, who claims to hear the voice of their deity directly. Zekuth wears crude religious symbols fashioned from bone and metal scraps and holds significant influence over the tribe's decisions.

**Situation:**

- Control the northern wing of the manor
- Recently captured a kobold wyrmling, holding it for ransom
- Running critically low on supplies, making them desperate and aggressive
- Planning a final assault on the kobold territory to secure an exit from the manor

**Goals:** Break free from the manor, spread their religious doctrine, and capture the "key" they believe the kobolds possess.

### The Kobolds

**Nature:** A clan of intelligent, cautious kobolds who have established a relatively peaceful community within the southern wing of the manor.

**Leadership:** Elder Vissk, a wise and pragmatic kobold shaman who negotiated the original arrangement with Vaelthas.

**Situation:**

- Have fortified the southern wing with clever traps and barricades
- Desperate to recover their captured wyrmling
- Guard a sealed tomb containing Madre Eudokia Doukas
- Possess a special key that maintains the magical seals on the tomb

**Goals:** Protect their wyrmling, maintain their guard duty over the sealed tomb, and survive the goblin threat.

### The Sealed Tomb

An ancient sarcophagus bound with arcane sigils, containing Madre Eudokia Doukas—rumored to be an oracle of tremendous power whose prophecies were deemed too dangerous. The kobolds only know what the cultists told them: that the tomb must be sealed at all costs. They are however, willing to negotiate with the characters to help them recover the wyrmling and defeat the goblins.

### Lord Blackthorne's Study

Once an elegant testament to scholarly pursuit, Lord Matthias Blackthorne's personal library now stands as a monument to obsession and necromantic corruption. Tall oak shelves line the walls, many now tilted precariously or collapsed entirely. The air hangs heavy with the scent of mold, parchment, and something far more sinister—the lingering essence of death magic.

Among the ruins, several dangerous items remain:

- **Necromantic Research Notes**: Meticulous records of Blackthorne's experiments in creating revenants, growing increasingly erratic over time. The final entries speak of "the perfect formula" and "immortality through undeath." References to "The Gravedigger's gifts" and "subjects from Black Hollow" reveal his collaboration with darker powers.

- **Forbidden Texts**: Several volumes bound in strange leather bear signs of extensive study, with notes in Blackthorne's increasingly unstable handwriting. These include texts on soul binding, a partial translation of "*Ithilnorith*" (Moon's Truth), and detailed diagrams of necromantic binding circles.

- **Convergence Maps**: Faded parchments show detailed mappings of ley lines beneath the manor, with particular focus on the ancient convergence point directly beneath the sealed tomb. Notes indicate this was the power source for his revenant creation rituals.

- **Family Correspondence**: Letters from concerned relatives questioning Blackthorne's "unhealthy experiments" and "disturbing changes in demeanor." The most recent letters mention missing servants and "unnatural sounds from the cellar." All went unanswered.

- **The Gravedigger's Letters**: Hidden among the papers are encrypted communications from The Gravedigger, discussing "suitable subjects," "ancient methods," and "the convergence point's potential." These reveal the true scope of their collaboration.

The kobolds avoid this room entirely, claiming the books "speak without sound" and "hunger for the living." The goblins, meanwhile, have attempted to pillage it for ritual components, though several of their number vanished after handling certain volumes—only to return days later as shambling undead.

A careful observer might notice that some of the books appear to have been recently consulted, and that the temperature in the room drops noticeably near the necromantic texts, as if death itself clings to them.
