# Level 02 - The Undercroft

## Overview

The manor's original basement level, now serving as an intermediate layer between the surface and the ancient ruins below. This level contains the family crypt (where the Hollow Lord now dwells), wine cellars, and the entrance to The Mines. Most of the valuable research has been stripped away, leaving only fragments of Lord Blackthorne's obsession.

## Environmental Features

- **Damp Stone**: Moisture seeps through cracked foundations
- **Musty Air**: Stagnant atmosphere with hints of decay
- **Uneven Floors**: Settling and magical corruption have warped the stonework
- **Cold Drafts**: Air currents from the ancient ruins below
- **Hidden entrance to deeper tunnels leading to The Mines**

## Room Descriptions

### The Storage Complex

The manor's basement storage was once a well-organized system of interconnected chambers serving different preservation and storage needs. Now it's a maze of abandoned rooms with signs of hasty evacuation and systematic looting.

#### The Wine Cellar

- **Size**: 30 feet × 20 feet (high vaulted ceiling, 12 feet)
- **Original Purpose**: Premium wine storage and aging
- **Layout**: Stone archways supporting wine racks, with a tasting area near the entrance
- **Current Condition**: 
  - Most bottles removed, but broken glass and wine stains cover the floor
  - Several racks overturned, suggesting hasty searching
  - A few remaining bottles of exceptional vintage (valuable but possibly spoiled)
- **Atmosphere**: Sweet-sour smell of spilled wine mixed with musty air
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - Secret door behind the largest oak wine rack (DC 15 Investigation)
  - Leads directly to the family crypt
  - Wine inventory ledger hidden in tasting table drawer shows recent unusual orders

#### The Root Cellar

- **Size**: 20 feet × 15 feet (low ceiling, 8 feet)
- **Original Purpose**: Long-term storage of vegetables, preserved foods, and dry goods
- **Layout**: Stone shelving carved into walls, with ventilation shafts and drainage channels
- **Current Condition**:
  - Rotted vegetables and spoiled preserves create a nauseating smell
  - Storage bins overturned and contents scattered
  - Evidence of vermin infestation (rats, insects)
- **Atmosphere**: Overwhelming decay, skittering sounds in the walls
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - Loose stone in wall conceals small cavity with silver coins (emergency fund)
  - Ventilation shaft leads to surface but is too narrow for human passage
  - Preserved food stores show signs of being rationed during some crisis

#### The Dry Storage Chambers

- **Size**: 25 feet × 30 feet (standard ceiling, 10 feet)
- **Original Purpose**: Grain, flour, salt, and other bulk dry goods
- **Layout**: Large stone bins and wooden casks arranged around central sorting area
- **Current Condition**:
  - Most containers emptied, grain scattered across floor
  - Signs of systematic removal - The Gravedigger's agents were methodical here
  - Water damage from broken pipes has ruined remaining stores
- **Atmosphere**: Musty, with sounds of dripping water echoing from damaged pipes
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - False bottom in largest grain bin contains wrapped documents (supply contracts mentioning suspicious purchases)
  - Tool marks show professional thieves worked here
  - Recent footprints in spilled flour suggest ongoing visits

#### The Cistern Chamber

- **Size**: 20 feet × 20 feet (high ceiling, 14 feet to accommodate cistern depth)
- **Original Purpose**: Rainwater collection and storage for the manor
- **Layout**: Large stone cistern with filtration system and distribution pipes
- **Current Condition**:
  - Water level dangerously low and stagnant
  - Filtration system clogged with debris and something organic
  - Cracks in cistern walls allow seepage
- **Atmosphere**: Echo of dripping, smell of stagnant water, unnaturally cold
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - Bottom of cistern contains bones and metal objects (victims thrown in?)
  - Pipe access tunnels could provide alternate routes but are partially flooded
  - Recent scratches on cistern edge suggest something large climbed out

#### The Coal Cellar

- **Size**: 15 feet × 20 feet (standard ceiling, 10 feet, with delivery chute extending upward)
- **Original Purpose**: Fuel storage for manor heating systems
- **Layout**: Reinforced stone chamber with chute from street level, coal bins
- **Current Condition**:
  - Coal mostly removed, leaving thick dust coating everything
  - Delivery chute sealed from above (potential entry/exit point)
  - Wooden support beams show signs of stress and possible collapse
- **Atmosphere**: Coal dust makes breathing difficult, muffled sounds
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - Chute could be cleared with effort, providing secret entrance
  - Coal dust preserves footprints - shows traffic patterns of past months
  - Small cavity behind loose stones contains miner's tools (hints at The Mines)

#### Connecting Passages

- **Layout**: Narrow stone corridors connect all storage areas
- **Current Condition**: Some passages partially collapsed or blocked by debris
- **Features**:
  - Service stairs to main manor (blocked)
  - Delivery access to manor grounds (sealed)
  - Connection to family crypt (hidden behind wine cellar)
  - Possible connection to The Mines entrance (requires investigation)

### The Servants' Quarters

- **Condition**: Abandoned hastily
- **Atmosphere**: Personal belongings scattered, signs of quick departure
- **Hidden Elements**:
  - Letters from servants describing strange noises
  - Evidence of growing fear among the household staff
  - Mentions of "digging sounds" at night

### The Mines Entrance

- **Condition**: Rough-hewn passage leading down to the ancient ruins
- **Atmosphere**: Tool marks, abandoned equipment, signs of hasty evacuation
- **Current State**: **Collapsed from recent tremors - the corruption of the forest has reached the naked walls. Corrupted vines and fungi have grown through the stone, causing structural instability and eventual collapse.**

## The Family Crypt

### Layout

- **Central Chamber**: Blackthorne family tombs arranged in a circle
- **Ritual Space**: Open area in the center, now used for necromantic rituals
- **Hidden Alcoves**: Secret compartments behind false walls
- **Ceiling**: Vaulted stone with family crests and heraldic symbols

### The Hollow Lord's Lair

- **Condition**: Transformed into a necromantic laboratory
- **Atmosphere**: Bones, ritual circles, alchemical equipment
- **Features**:
  - Ancestor tomb converted into ritual altar
  - Stacks of bodies in various stages of reanimation
  - Alchemical apparatus (mostly broken from his mindless state)
  - Personal effects from his former life (triggers for roleplay)

### Environmental Hazards

- **Necrotic Energy**: Constant exposure to undead magic
- **Structural Instability**: Some areas partially collapsed from unstable construction
- **Toxic Vapors**: Alchemical residue creates poisonous gases
- **Unstable Magic**: Wild magic surges from failed experiments

## Hidden Discoveries

### Research Fragments

- **Condition**: Scattered notes, mostly ruined or incomplete
- **Content**:
  - Rough sketches of ritual patterns
  - Failed experiment records
  - Fragments of correspondence with "Magistra Ashfeld"
  - Evidence of growing desperation and fragmented dreams

### The Gravedigger's Theft

- **Evidence**: Systematic removal of valuable research
- **Methods**: Tool marks, careful extraction, professional approach
- **Timeline**: Occurred shortly after Lord Blackthorne's transformation
- **Scope**: Most useful necromantic knowledge and artifacts removed

### Lord Blackthorne's Obsession

- **Focus**: Seeking "the ancient library" and obtaining Ithilnorith
- **Methods**: Following dream guidance rather than systematic research
- **Transformation**: The Mines leads down to the ancient ruins
- **Failure**: His final transformation attempt went catastrophically wrong

## Combat Encounters

### The Hollow Lord Boss Fight

- **Location**: Family crypt central chamber
- **Tactics**: Summons undead minions, uses environmental hazards
- **Phases**:
  - Initial assault with skeleton warriors
  - Necromantic spells and life drain attacks
  - Desperate final phase with self-destructive magic
- **Victory Conditions**: Destroying ritual components, purifying the chamber

### Environmental Challenges

- **Structural Collapse**: Damaged areas can cave in during combat
- **Toxic Gases**: Alchemical residue creates hazardous zones
- **Necromantic Traps**: Magical wards left from Lord Blackthorne's experiments
- **Undead Ambushes**: Mindless servants attack from hidden alcoves

## Key Information

### What the PCs Can Learn

- **The Transformation**: Evidence of Lord Blackthorne's failed experiment
- **The Gravedigger's Involvement**: Signs of systematic theft and manipulation
- **The Ancient Ruins**: Connection between the manor and the deeper mysteries
- **The Collapse**: Recent tremors and forest corruption caused The Mines to collapse

### What Leads to the Next Level

- **The Mines**: Passage to the ancient ruins (currently collapsed)
- **Alternative Routes**: Other entrances to the deeper levels may exist
- **The Gravedigger's Network**: Clues about how to find her other operations
- **Ancient Artifacts**: Items that might provide access to the deeper mysteries

## Campaign Integration

### Connection to The Gravedigger

- **Historical Partnership**: Evidence of her manipulation of Lord Blackthorne
- **Current Operations**: This level serves as a testing ground for her undead servants
- **Strategic Value**: The Hollow Lord guards the route to the ancient ruins
- **Future Threat**: Her agents may return to reclaim additional resources

### Connection to the Deeper Mystery

- **Ancient Ruins**: The Mines provides (when cleared) access to the deeper levels
- **Aboleth Influence**: Signs of otherworldly corruption throughout the area
- **Artifact Clues**: References to Ithilnorith and other items of power
- **Historical Significance**: Evidence of the manor's connection to lost civilizations

### Ongoing Threats

- **The Hollow Lord**: Continues to create undead servants
- **Structural Instability**: The level is gradually becoming more dangerous
- **Corruption Spread**: The influence is slowly expanding upward
- **The Mines Collapse**: Prevents easy access to the deeper levels but may be cleared with effort

The Undercroft represents the **transition point** between the surface mystery and the deeper campaign. It provides crucial information about the past while setting up the challenges to come. The Hollow Lord's defeat here should feel like a significant victory, but also reveal that the true threat lies deeper beneath the manor.
