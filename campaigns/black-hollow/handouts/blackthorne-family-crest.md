# The Blackthorne Family Crest

## **Heraldic Description**

**Original Crest (Uncorrupted):**

- **Shield:** Azure (blue) field representing the northern seas
- **Chief Element:** A silver lighthouse tower rising from stylized waves
- **Base Element:** Three white salt crystals arranged in triangle formation
- **Border:** Silver saltire (diagonal cross) representing the salt trade routes
- **Motto:** *"Lux et Sal"* (Light and Salt) - written in Imperial Latin below the shield

## **Symbolism**

### **The Lighthouse**

- Represents the Blackthorne family's role as guides and protectors of Black Hollow
- Maritime heritage as "Barons of Salt" controlling northern sea trade
- Metaphor for bringing light to dark places (tragically ironic given Lord Blackthorne's fate)

### **The Salt Crystals**

- Direct reference to their title as "Barons of Salt"
- Represents purity, preservation, and prosperity through trade
- White color symbolizes nobility and honor

### **The Blue Field**

- Represents the Sea of the North that brought their wealth
- Symbol of depth, wisdom, and stability
- Azure is a noble color in heraldry

### **The Silver Saltire**

- Represents the crossroads of trade routes they controlled
- Silver indicates purity and truth in heraldic tradition
- Forms the backbone of their commercial empire

## **Corrupted Version (Current State)**

After Lord Blackthorne's transformation into the Hollow Lord, the family crest has been twisted by necromantic energies:

### **Physical Corruption**

- **The lighthouse** appears cracked and bleeding a dark substance
- **The salt crystals** have turned black and seem to writhe like living things
- **The blue field** has darkened to an unnatural deep purple-black
- **The silver saltire** is now tarnished and appears to be binding chains
- **The motto** remains but the letters seem to shift and blur when observed directly

### **Supernatural Effects**

- The crest appears to move slightly when viewed peripherally
- Metal versions (like on the collar) emit a faint cold light
- Looking directly at corrupted versions causes brief feelings of dread
- The lighthouse occasionally flickers with sickly green light

## **Historical Usage**

- Carved into the gates of the original Blackthorne Manor
- Stamped on official documents and trade agreements
- Worn as signet rings by family members
- Displayed on banners during formal occasions
- Incorporated into the family tomb designs

## **Current State**

Most representations of the Blackthorne crest in Black Hollow show signs of corruption. The family's fall from grace has literally twisted their heraldic symbols, making them objects of unease rather than pride. Only the most protected or blessed representations (such as those in holy ground) retain their original appearance.
