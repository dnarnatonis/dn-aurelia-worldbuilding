# Skeletal Bearer

In life, the Bone Bearer was a banner-bearer whose sole purpose was to carry the war flag with the sigil of Lord Blackthorne's house into battle; carrying the Banner high for all to see, to inspire and uplift the morale of the troops. The Hollow Lord, however, has little use for a banner-bearer, for the dead don't need moral support, nor do they need to be inspired to fight. However, the banner that the skeleton still carries bears the family sigil of the one who killed him and subsequently resurrected him. Just like he was turned into a mockery of life and death, so shall he turn the Blackthorne family's sigil into such a mockery. The flag, infused with a fragment of the Hollow Lord's terrible will and power, grants the Bone Bearer the power to reanimate the fallen and empower its unholy allies with terrible strength.

The Bone Bearer is an enemy that appears only in the deepest expeditions within the Manor Ruins. While it remains standing, the banner it bears will inspire any nearby undead to fight with increased strength, and its presence may imbue individual allies, living or undead, with a surge of power. Should its allies fall in battle, the Bone Bearer will reanimate them, forcing heroes to fight and kill them again and again.

The Bone Bearer serves as a rally-point for groups of lesser skeletons. While physically weak, the Bearer is nonetheless a formidable threat due to its ability to apply layer upon layer of dark blessings to other nearby enemies. The Bone Bearer's presence provides all nearby undead with enhanced combat prowess, with further empowerment provided each turn through its wicked incantations, turning any and potentially all of its allies into highly dangerous threats.

The most significant of the Bearer's abilities, however, is its power to recall the fallen; this dark ritual is performed as the battle rages, and returns fallen corpses to unlife at full strength, forcing adventurers to fight them all over again. Even wounds and ailments that had weakened the corpse before its destruction are removed as the dark magic restores it completely.

If forced to the front line, the Bearer will desperately attempt to return to the back ranks where it can better coordinate its unholy allies, using its corrupted banner as both weapon and shield in its retreat.

**Found in:** The Manor Ruins (Deepest Levels)
