# The Hollow Lord

## Overview

**Name:** Lord Matthias Blackthorne (formerly), now known only as "The Hollow Lord"

**Job:** Corrupted necromancer and undead abomination

**Goal:** None (mindless) - driven only by an insatiable hunger to raise and command the dead

**Use:** Final boss of the Fallen Manor; represents the consequences of unchecked ambition and forbidden knowledge

**Quirk:** Speaks in fragments of multiple voices - his own, his family members, and the undead he has consumed

## Background

Lord Matthias Blackthorne was once a respected nobleman and scholar, but his insatiable curiosity about death and undeath led him down a dark path. Working in secret collaboration with The Gravedigger's network, he became obsessed with perfecting the art of creating revenants - undead beings that retain their intelligence and memories while being bound to serve their creator.

### The Manipulation

The Gravedigger, always seeking new methods to further her true agenda, carefully manipulated Blackthorne into beginning his excavations. Using her academic credentials as **Magistra (Emerita) Morwyn Ashfeld**, she commissioned and delivered the archaeological report "The Temple of Harmonious Movement" directly to Blackthorne. This document, filled with tantalizing hints about ancient power beneath his manor, was precisely crafted to appeal to his scholarly curiosity and ambition.

The Gravedigger provided him with:

- The manipulated archaeological report that convinced him to excavate
- Ancient texts on necromancy and undeath (gradually introduced)
- Rare components for his experiments
- Test subjects from Black Hollow's forgotten souls
- Protection from imperial authorities

In exchange, Blackthorne shared his research and provided The Gravedigger with:

- Perfected methods for creating intelligent undead
- A network of undead servants throughout the region
- Access to the ancient convergence point beneath his manor
- Knowledge of the sealed powers in the deeper crypts

### The Perfected Method

Blackthorne's breakthrough came when he discovered how to bind a soul to its corpse using a combination of:

- **Sacrificial rituals** (provided by The Gravedigger's cult knowledge)
- **Ancient binding circles** (carved into the manor's foundation)
- **Personal anchors** (items of deep emotional significance to the target)

This process created revenants that were:

- Intellectually intact
- Emotionally driven by their last strong desire
- Physically enhanced beyond normal undead
- Completely loyal to their creator

### The Circle of Corruption

As Blackthorne's experiments progressed, he attracted others who would become entwined in his dark work:

**Althaea Wintermoon**: A brilliant High Elf scholar from distant lands who came seeking ancient knowledge. What began as academic collaboration blossomed into deep love. She worked alongside Matthias for years, studying necromantic rituals and lunar magic. When he attempted his final transformation, she was present - conducting the ritual herself, believing their love would anchor his humanity. Instead, the corrupted ritual shattered his mind, and in his first moments as the Hollow Lord, he killed her. Her spirit, unable to accept this betrayal, now haunts Eelgrave as a banshee, forever mourning the man she loved.

**Madre Eudokia Doukas**: The oracle visited Blackthorne several times, drawn by whispers of ancient knowledge. Each visit changed her little by little as she was exposed to the convergence point's power and the growing Aboleth influence. She began having visions - first glimpses of truth, then increasingly disturbing revelations. One night, she simply vanished into the woods, only to be found later by the Cult of Vaelthas, who recognized her value as a vessel for otherworldly visions.

### The Fatal Experiment

Drunk on his success, Blackthorne attempted to apply his perfected method to himself, believing he could become an immortal undead lord while retaining his full intelligence and power. However, the process went catastrophically wrong:

1. **The Ritual Began** - Blackthorne performed the ceremony in the deepest crypt, surrounded by his undead servants
2. **The Corruption Spread** - The lunar essence, tainted by the deeper corruption beneath the manor, overwhelmed his soul
3. **The Mind Shattered** - Instead of preserving his intelligence, the ritual fractured his consciousness into fragments
4. **The Hunger Awakened** - He became an insatiable devourer of souls, consuming his own family and servants
5. **The Transformation Completed** - He emerged as a mindless necromantic abomination, driven only by the need to create more undead

### The Gravedigger's Harvest

Once Blackthorne had transformed into the Hollow Lord, The Gravedigger stepped in to reap her rewards. With her former collaborator now mindless and contained within the manor, she was free to:

- **Secure his research**: All his perfected methods for creating revenants now belonged to her
- **Claim the convergence point**: The ancient power source beneath the manor became her private resource
- **Ensure containment**: She sealed the manor and allowed it to be forgotten, creating the perfect cover story
- **Exploit the network**: The undead servants Blackthorne had created became her agents throughout the region
- **Let him guard the secrets**: The mindless Hollow Lord now unknowingly protects the deeper mysteries from anyone who might threaten her plans

This arrangement serves The Gravedigger perfectly - she gained everything she needed while her former partner became an unwitting guardian of the very power she now exploits.

## Current State

The Hollow Lord now exists as a grotesque parody of his former self:

**Physical Description:**

- Tall, gaunt figure draped in tattered nobleman's robes
- Hollow eye sockets that glow with shifting lunar light
- Skeletal hands that drip with necrotic energy
- Crown of bone fragments from his consumed family
- Moves with jerky, unnatural motions

**Mental State:**

- Completely mindless, driven only by instinct
- Speaks in fragments - his own voice mixed with those he's consumed
- Recognizes no one, attacks anything living on sight
- Continues his necromantic rituals through muscle memory alone

**Powers:**

- Master of necromancy (though applied mindlessly)
- Can raise any corpse within the manor instantly
- Commands all undead in the area
- Regenerates by consuming soul energy
- Can create new revenants through instinctive ritual

## Resources

### What He Controls

- Ancient necromantic knowledge (fragmented)
- Access to the deeper ruins beneath his manor
- Network of undead servants
- Family crypt and ancestral holdings

### What He Seeks

- **Lunar essence** (no longer available after his transformation)
- More souls to fuel his experiments
- Access to the deeper chambers he can no longer reach
- The lost knowledge of proper revenant creation

## Connection to The Gravedigger

The Hollow Lord represents one of **The Gravedigger's** early attempts to establish a network of necromantic collaborators. Before his transformation, Lord Matthias Blackthorne was approached by **Magistra (Emerita) Morwyn Ashfeld** with an archaeological opportunity that seemed too good to refuse.

### The Manipulation

- **The Gravedigger** used her academic credentials to gain Blackthorne's trust
- She provided him with the "Temple of Harmonious Movement" report, carefully edited to encourage excavation
- As Blackthorne's obsession grew, she gradually introduced him to necromantic theories
- The relationship changed little by little as she was exposed to the ancient ruins' power and the growing Aboleth influence. She began having visions - first glimpses of truth, then increasingly disturbing revelations. One night, she simply vanished into the woods

### The Betrayal

- When Blackthorne's final experiment failed, **The Gravedigger** moved quickly
- She claimed his research, his methods, and his accumulated knowledge
- **Claim the ancient ruins**: The power source beneath the manor became her private resource
- She left him as an unwitting guardian of the deeper secrets

## Story Hooks

### For Investigation

1. **Ancient Research Notes** - Fragments of correspondence between Blackthorne and "Magistra Ashfeld"
2. **The Failed Experiment** - Evidence of what went wrong during his transformation attempt
3. **The Abandoned Partnership** - Signs that someone else was involved in his research
4. **A Lost Opportunity** - The knowledge he possessed about the ancient ruins and deeper mysteries is now locked away in his fractured mind

### For Encounters

1. **The Mindless Guardian** - He attacks anything that approaches his crypt
2. **Echoes of the Past** - Moments of clarity where he speaks of his former research
3. **The Revenant Factory** - His compulsive creation of undead servants
4. **The Protective Barrier** - His presence actually prevents others from accessing the deeper levels

## Weaknesses

### Combat Weaknesses

- **Mindless Nature**: Can be manipulated or distracted by clever tactics
- **Ritual Dependence**: Destroying his necromantic components prevents regeneration
- **Confined Territory**: Rarely leaves his crypt, making him predictable
- **Aboleth Influence**: Vulnerable to effects that disrupt otherworldly connections

### Story Weaknesses

- **Lost Knowledge**: Cannot remember crucial details about the deeper mysteries
- **Broken Network**: His transformation severed his connection to **The Gravedigger**
- **Purifying the ancient ruins** prevents his regeneration
- **Isolation**: No allies or support beyond his mindless undead

## Roleplaying Notes

### Personality Traits

- **Obsessive**: Repeats the same necromantic rituals endlessly
- **Fragmented**: Brief moments of clarity reveal his former self
- **Protective**: Violently guards his crypt and belongings
- **Tragic**: Glimpses of what he once was make him pitiable

### Speech Patterns

- Mostly unintelligible muttering about "the deep chambers" and "the perfect method"
- Occasional lucid statements about his research
- Repetitive phrases about transformation and immortality
- Fragments of conversations with "Magistra Ashfeld"
