## The Schism of the Three Queens

The **Three Queens** were legendary figures whose decisions during the **Age of Heroes** shaped the destiny of the Elves. Each queen’s vision reflected different interpretations of their race’s purpose, creating a rift that led to the formation of the **Wood Elves**, **High Elves**, and **Dark Elves**.

### The Wood Elves: Guardians of Balance  

- **Queen**: **Sylphariel**, the Verdant Sentinel  
- **Philosophy**: The Wood Elves followed **Sylphariel**, who believed the Elves’ duty was to maintain the natural balance of the Material Plane as ordained by Viridiana. They saw themselves as stewards of nature and defenders of harmony.  
- **Culture**: The Wood Elves lived in deep forests, their cities blending seamlessly with the natural world. They revered the Classical Gods, especially **Lothiriel**, **Thalvok**, and **Sylvara**. Their magic was primal and druidic, rooted in the cycles of life.  
- **Conflict**: Sylphariel condemned the ambitions of her sisters, viewing their paths as betrayals of Viridiana’s will.  

---

### The High Elves: Seekers of Dominion  

- **Queen**: **Elathis Araenor**, the Moonlit Sovereign  
- **Philosophy**: Under **Elathis**, the High Elves believed their inherent superiority demanded they lead through civilization and mastery of the arcane arts. They allied with the **Outer God of Secrets and the Moon**, known in Abyssal as **Ulmathar** and in Elvish as **Ithilthar**.  
- **Culture**: High Elves built grand cities of marble and crystal, their architecture reflecting their reverence for knowledge and celestial power. They saw the moon as a symbol of enlightenment and mystery, guiding their pursuit of magic.  
- **Conflict**: The High Elves sought to expand their dominion, viewing themselves as the rightful rulers of the world. Their alliance with **Ulmathar** granted them powerful secrets, but it deepened the rift with the other Elves.

---

### The Dark Elves: Rulers by Might  

- **Queen**: **Kaelyth Draevor**, the Shadowed Blade  
- **Philosophy**: **Kaelyth** rejected the ideals of balance and enlightenment, believing the Elves should rule through strength and fear. She allied with a **Lesser God of the Hells**, who offered power in exchange for loyalty.  
- **Culture**: The Dark Elves moved underground, carving cities in vast caverns illuminated by bioluminescent fungi. They worshipped their infernal patron and prized martial prowess, shadow magic, and cunning.  
- **Conflict**: Kaelyth’s path led to a break with her sisters, and her followers became known for their ruthlessness and ambition. The Dark Elves saw domination as the only way to ensure their survival and supremacy.

---

### The Breaking of Elven Unity  

The **Schism of the Three Queens** began as philosophical disagreements but erupted into open conflict. Key events include:

1. **The Council of Tharathien**: An attempt to reconcile the queens ended in betrayal and bloodshed, marking the formal split of the Elves.  
2. **The Battle of Moonglade**: The High Elves and Wood Elves clashed over control of sacred glades tied to Viridiana’s power, with neither side achieving a decisive victory.  
3. **The Shadowfall Betrayal**: Kaelyth’s forces ambushed a coalition of Wood Elves and High Elves, driving a permanent wedge between the factions.

---

### Legacy of the Schism  

The schism left deep scars on Elven culture:  

- **Wood Elves** retreated to the forests, isolating themselves to protect nature.  
- **High Elves** became imperialists, expanding their influence and building a legacy of magical innovation.  
- **Dark Elves** were vilified, becoming the boogeymen of Elven myths and the source of mortal fear.
