# **Umbral Expanse**

The **Umbral Expanse** is one of the primordial planes created during Viridiana's shaping of reality. When she sacrificed her Will to create mortal souls, the act split the ethereal realm into two reflections: the vibrant **Feyglade Veil** and the shadowed **Umbral Expanse**. Where the Feyglade embodies the joy and chaos of life, the Umbral Expanse represents its inevitable end - a realm of twilight where echoes of mortal souls linger before their return to the cycle.

This duality was intentional - for in Viridiana's wisdom, she knew that both light and shadow were necessary for the great cycle of existence. The Umbral Expanse serves as both warning and necessity, a place where the weight of mortality can be felt and understood.

---

## **Origins and Nature**

The Umbral Expanse exists as a dark reflection of the Material Plane, but its purpose is far more fundamental than mere mirroring:

- **The Weight of Mortality:** When Viridiana gave mortals their souls and free will, she created the Umbral Expanse as a repository for their fears, regrets, and shadows - aspects necessary for true consciousness
- **The Cycle's Shadow:** Just as the Feyglade Veil represents the wild potential of life, the Umbral Expanse embodies the necessity of endings and renewal
- **Memory's Echo:** The realm holds fragments of mortal memories and experiences, preserving them even as souls move through the cycle of rebirth

---

## **Geography**

The geography of the Umbral Expanse mirrors the Material Plane but is shaped by the collective consciousness of mortal souls:

1. **The Shattered Peaks:** Mountains formed from crystallized memories, their jagged spires echoing with the whispers of countless lives. Ancient wisdom and forgotten knowledge drift like mist through their valleys.

2. **The Twilight Plains:** Vast stretches where reality grows thin, and the boundaries between memory and present blur. Here, echoes of great battles, lost cities, and forgotten kingdoms play out in endless cycles.

3. **The Sea of Regrets:** A vast, dark ocean whose depths hold the weight of mortal sorrows. Its waters reflect not the sky but the deepest memories of those who gaze into them, showing paths not taken and choices unmade.

4. **The Halls of Reflection:** Natural formations where the veil between the Material Plane and the Umbral Expanse grows thin, allowing mortals rare glimpses of truth through dreams and meditation.

---

## **Denizens of the Umbral Expanse**

The realm is home to beings born from the essence of mortality itself:

- **Memory Wraiths:** Fragments of consciousness that retain the shape of their former lives, neither truly alive nor dead, but existing in a state of perpetual remembrance
- **The Forgotten:** Ancient entities formed from discarded memories and lost knowledge, keeping watch over the realm's deepest secrets
- **Echo Walkers:** Mortals who have learned to traverse the boundaries between planes, seeking wisdom in the shadows
- **The Twilight Court:** A mysterious gathering of entities who maintain the balance between memory and oblivion

---

## **Magic and Influence**

1. **Shadow Essence:** The realm's very nature amplifies magic tied to memory, dreams, and the passage of time. However, such power comes with the risk of becoming lost in the echoes of the past.

2. **Dream Walking:** Those with strong will or proper training can enter the Umbral Expanse through dreams, though this practice carries the danger of becoming trapped in memory.

3. **The Weight of Time:** Time flows differently in the Expanse, shaped by the intensity of memories rather than linear progression. A moment might stretch for an eternity, while centuries pass in the blink of an eye.

4. **Mortal Connection:** All mortal souls leave traces in the Umbral Expanse, creating a vast tapestry of interconnected memories and experiences that shape the realm's ever-changing landscape.

The Umbral Expanse stands as a testament to Viridiana's wisdom - a reminder that shadow is not evil, but a necessary part of existence. It is a place of endings and beginnings, where the weight of mortality gives meaning to life itself.
