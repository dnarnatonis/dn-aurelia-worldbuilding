# The Doomforged

Among the proud Silver Mountain Dwarves exists a solemn brotherhood of warriors known as the **Doomforged** (formally), or **Oath-Sworn** (**_Khazad-Drengi_** in their tongue). These grim fighters represent the ultimate expression of the dwarven honor code—those who have suffered unbearable shame and seek redemption through glorious death in battle.

## Origins and Purpose

### The Path of Dishonor

- **Irredeemable Transgressions**: When a dwarf breaks a sacred oath, fails to protect kin, loses an ancestral artifact, or suffers shame too great to bear, they stand before the Stonekeepers to receive judgment
- **The Doom Choice**: Rather than face exile or having their name chiseled from history (the true death), they may choose to become Doomforged
- **Cultural Necessity**: This tradition prevents blood feuds while upholding the sanctity of oaths that form the foundation of dwarven society

### The Sacred Oath of Doom

- **The Ritual**: Performed at the Deep Shrine within Silverdeep Hold where the dwarf swears upon the Eternal Anvil
- **The Transformation**: Hair is shaved except for a single crest dyed blood-red with mineral pigments from the sacred veins of the Silver Mountains
- **The Vow**: "Without honor I stand before the Anvil. Let my spirit be forged anew in battle's fire. I shall not return to the Deep Halls until my shame is washed away by glory or by death."

## Hierarchy of the Doomforged

The longer a Doomforged survives, the more impressive their deeds must become to achieve redemption:

- **Stone-Sworn**: Newly-oathed warriors seeking dangerous foes
- **Deep-Sworn**: Veterans who have slain powerful beasts like cave trolls
- **Mountain-Sworn**: Elite warriors who have defeated giants or other mighty opponents
- **Star-Sworn**: Legendary fighters who have survived battles against dragons, demons, or servants of the Old Gods

With each level, the Doomforged's red crest grows longer, becoming both a mark of impressive survival and a symbol of continued failure to achieve redemption.

## Visual Identity

- **Blood-Crest**: The distinctive red mohawk that grows with each quest for death
- **Rune-Marked Skin**: Ritual scarification performed by Runepriests, recording both shame and glory
- **Minimal Armor**: Often wearing only enough protection to keep fighting, displaying battle scars as badges of honor
- **Paired Weapons**: Usually axes or hammers, often inscribed with personal oaths and ancestral runes

## Role in Dwarven Society

Though technically outcasts, the Doomforged serve crucial functions within Silver Mountain culture:

- **Vanguard Defenders**: First into battle when holds face overwhelming threats
- **Monster Hunters**: Seeking out remnants of the Old Gods' corruption as part of their death-quest
- **Border Wardens**: Many patrol the dangerous frontiers between the Empire and Silver Host
- **Living Warnings**: Each represents a cautionary tale about the price of broken oaths

### The Cultural Paradox

- Doomforged are simultaneously outcast yet deeply respected
- They cannot be invited to feast halls but are always welcome at the threshold
- Their deeds are recorded by Stonekeepers though their pre-oath names are struck from regular records
- They embody Khorvyn's (Duragoth's) teaching that shame can be the forge that transforms weakness into strength

## Sacred Places

- **The Hall of Final Testimony**: Deep within Silverdeep Hold, where the walls are inscribed with the deeds of fallen Doomforged whose honor was restored
- **The Red Forge**: Special smithy where Doomforged craft or enhance their weapons, heated by fires said to contain essence from the Eternal Anvil
- **The Seven Peaks**: Sacred mountains where particularly legendary Doomforged have met their end in glorious battle

## Cultural Traditions

### The Three Bindings

Every Doomforged makes three vows upon the stone:

1. To seek a death worthy of remembrance
2. To protect innocent dwarves despite personal shame
3. To never speak of the dishonor that set them on this path

### Death and Redemption

- If a Doomforged achieves a sufficiently glorious death, their soul is guided by Stonekeepers to the Deep Halls
- Their red crest is cut and preserved in the Hall of Final Testimony
- Their original name may be restored to clan records if their redemption is deemed complete

## Notable Doomforged

- **Grimhammer the Unyielding**: A Mountain-Sworn who has slain seventeen trolls and three giants, yet still lives to his frustration
- **Runeheart the Vigilant**: Female Deep-Sworn who guards the treacherous pass between the Silver Host and the Empire
- **Ironbrow the Cursed**: Legendary Star-Sworn who survived battle with a servant of Ulthrex, now believed to be over 300 years old
- **The Cinder Company**: Rare band of Doomforged who fight together, all having taken the oath after failing to prevent a mine collapse

## Current Challenges

- **Silver Host Recruitment**: The zealous Silver Host attempts to recruit Doomforged as elite shock troops against the Empire
- **Ancestral Ruins**: The fallen halls of Dur-Khazad offer tempting death-grounds filled with ancient horrors
- **Erythril Madness**: Some Doomforged deliberately seek out Erythril-tainted areas, believing the red metal's corruption will fuel their battle rage
- **The Endless Path**: Some Doomforged survive decades despite seeking death, leading them to question whether ancestral spirits have other plans for them

## Integration with Dwarven Faith

The Doomforged tradition stands as a living embodiment of several core dwarven beliefs:

- They represent the ultimate test of the **Stone Oath** tradition
- Their path follows the teaching that **"Craft is Worship"** by transforming themselves into weapons
- They embrace the principle that **"to be forgotten is a fate worse than death"** by seeking memorable ends
- Some Stonekeepers view the Doomforged as the physical manifestation of **"The Doom of the Forgotten"** - a warning to all dwarves of the price of dishonor

In the complex political landscape between the Empire and the Silver Host, the Doomforged often find themselves as unexpected diplomats, their outcast status allowing them to move between factions and speak uncomfortable truths that clan-bound dwarves cannot.
