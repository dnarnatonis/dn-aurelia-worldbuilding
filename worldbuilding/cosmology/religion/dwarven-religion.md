# **Dwarven Religion in the Aurelian Empire**  

*(A faith built on stone, ancestry, and unyielding duty.)*  

Dwarven religion is **not centered around gods in the traditional sense**. Instead, it revolves around **ancestral reverence, the eternal nature of stone, and the cosmic cycle of creation and entropy**. Their faith is deeply **pragmatic, ritualistic, and tied to craftsmanship, honor, and legacy**.  

---

## **1. Core Beliefs of the Dwarves**

- **The Stone is Eternal:** The world was **forged from primal stone**, and all living beings are **impermanent veins within it.**  
- **Ancestry is the Foundation:** The deeds of one’s **forebears shape the future**, and **to be forgotten is a fate worse than death.**  
- **Craft is Worship:** Every **act of creation—smithing, masonry, engineering—is a holy act**, binding the dwarf to their ancestors.  
- **Oaths Are Sacred:** **A spoken promise is an unbreakable bond**, and oathbreakers are **cursed to fade from history.**  

---

## **2. The Pillars of the Faith**

### **A. The Eternal Anvil – The Heart of Creation**

- The **cosmic forge where all things are shaped**.  
- When a dwarf dies, their spirit **returns to the Anvil**, where they are reforged into something new.  
- **Great ancestors do not reincarnate but become part of the stone, forever watching over their kin.**  

### **B. The Deep Halls – The Afterlife of the Worthy**

- **Dwarves who die with honor** are welcomed into **the Deep Halls**, an endless underworld of **stone, halls, and hearths**, where they **feast, craft, and counsel their descendants through dreams.**  
- **Those who die as oathbreakers or cowards do not enter the Halls.** Instead, **their names are chiseled from history**, and their souls dissolve into nothingness.  

### **C. The Doom of the Forgotten – The Great Fear**

- To be **forgotten is the ultimate punishment.**  
- Dwarves believe **true death comes not when the body fails, but when the last person who remembers your name is gone.**  
- The worst criminals are **stripped of their clan, their name, and their history, condemned to the Void.**  

---

## **3. The Keepers of the Faith**

### **A. The Stonekeepers (Priests & Loremasters)**

- **Hardened scholars, warriors, and record-keepers.**  
- **Do not “pray” in the human sense**—instead, they **preserve history, enforce oaths, and ensure the dead are remembered.**  
- **Masters of funerary rites**, ensuring **souls are guided to the Deep Halls.**  

### **B. The Runepriests (Mystics & Guardians)**

- **Engrave sacred runes of protection, strength, and wisdom.**  
- **Can awaken ancestral echoes**—spirits of the dead that whisper guidance.  
- Some say **the oldest runepriests can shape the very stone with their words.**  

### **C. The Doomforged (Oathbound Exiles)**

- **Dwarves who take self-imposed exile to cleanse a blood debt.**  
- **They seek redemption through great deeds**, hoping to reclaim their name.  
- Feared and respected, as **they have no home to return to.**  

---

## **4. Major Religious Practices**

### **A. The Rite of the First Forge**

- **Every dwarf crafts something at their coming of age**—a weapon, tool, or work of art.  
- If they fail, **they are given one more chance.** If they fail again, **they are exiled.**  

### **B. Oathbinding Ceremonies**

- **Swearing an oath is a lifelong commitment.**  
- If a dwarf breaks an oath, **they must either atone or accept exile.**  

### **C. The Ritual of Stone-Telling (Funeral Rite)**

- The dead are **laid to rest in stone crypts, their deeds carved into their tombs.**  
- **Their name is spoken three times, ensuring they are never forgotten.**  
- The greatest dwarves are **entombed within the mountains, their spirits said to watch over their kin.**  

---

## **5. Relationship with Other Faiths**

- **Dwarves respect the Classical Gods of the Empire**, but they **do not worship them**—they are seen as **mortal concerns.**  
- **Auriana is respected, but seen as distant**—order is important, but **stone endures longer than empires.**  
- **They distrust chaotic deities and demons**, as they represent **the forces of decay and ruin.**  
- Some dwarves **secretly seek audience with the Old Gods**, believing they **hold the lost knowledge of the First Forge.**  

---

## **6. Theological Conflicts & Secrets**

- **What happens when the Deep Halls grow too full?** Some believe **the dwarves must forge a new world to house their ancestors.**  
- **The Black Forge—A Myth or a Warning?** Legends speak of **a dark anvil, where souls are twisted into cursed weapons.**  
- **Are the Oldest Dwarves Truly Dead?** Some say **the greatest ancestors still walk, hidden deep beneath the mountains, waiting for the end of the world.**
