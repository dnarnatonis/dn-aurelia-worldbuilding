# Aran'keth, City of the Gods (Ancient Elvish: *"Crown of Stone"*)

*"In dreams, I see twin towers spiraling into starlight, gates that whisper forgotten names, and streets where shadows remember the footsteps of gods."*
— From an ancient elvish text discovered in the Blackthorne Manor excavation

## 🌌 Origins & Divine Purpose

### The Seat of the Eight

During the **Age of Balance**, when Viridiana's Eight Lesser Gods walked the Material Plane, they established Aran'keth as their earthly seat. Built upon a convergence of ley lines where the veil between planes grows thin, the city served as both sanctuary and school for the mortal races.

The **Eight Throne Chambers** were carved into the living rock of Mount Aethros, each reflecting its god's domain:

- **Zaralon's Forge**: Eternal flames that taught dwarves metalworking
- **Astrael's Observatory**: Star-chambers where elven astronomy was born  
- **Sylvara's Garden**: Living halls where the secrets of growth were shared
- **Thalvok's Den**: Wild chambers where beast-speech was learned
- **Morthys' Sanctum**: Silent halls where the rites of peaceful death were taught
- **Voryn's Vaults**: Shadow-mazes where the art of secrets was mastered
- **Elyndra's Court**: Golden halls where justice and law were codified
- **Khorvyn's Depths**: Stone-workshops where gem-craft was perfected

### The Elven Guise

The Lesser Gods appeared primarily in elven form, their immortal beauty and wisdom making them natural teachers to the nascent races. This is why elven culture bears such strong divine influence, and why the city's architecture blends mortal craft with otherworldly geometry. The ancient elvish language became the sacred tongue of the city, used in all divine inscriptions and ceremonial rites.

## ⚡ The Age of Guidance

### The Divine Tutors

For centuries, the Eight walked among mortals:

- **Dwarven Clans** learned forge-craft from Zaralon and stone-mastery from Khorvyn
- **Elven Houses** studied star-lore with Astrael and life-mysteries with Sylvara  
- **Human Tribes** received teachings of justice from Elyndra and survival from Thalvok
- **All Races** learned to honor death through Morthys and navigate secrets through Voryn

### The Golden Age

The city flourished as a cosmopolitan center where all races mingled freely. The **Great Library of Astrael** contained knowledge from across the planes. The **Gardens of Sylvara** grew impossible plants that could heal any wound. The **Courts of Elyndra** settled disputes with divine wisdom.

## ⏳ The Fracturing

### The Withdrawal

As the Age of Balance waned, the Lesser Gods began to withdraw from direct involvement in mortal affairs. One by one, they retreated to their divine realms, leaving only echoes of their presence. The city began to change, reality becoming unstable without their constant divine influence.

### Temporal Schism

The massive magical energies that had sustained the city for millennia created a **temporal fracture**. Aran'keth now exists in two states simultaneously:

**Past Aran'keth** (*"The Golden Crown"*): The city as it was during the divine age - vibrant, whole, filled with impossible beauty and the presence of the gods.

**Present Aran'keth** (*"The Broken Crown"*): The ruins as they exist now - crumbling stone, twisted passages, and reality-tears where divine magic once flowed.

## 🏙️ The Ruined City

### Current State

What remains is a labyrinthine ruin buried deep beneath the Blackthorne Manor, its architecture defying mortal understanding. Twin towers still spiral impossibly high through natural caverns, their peaks lost in the stone ceiling above. Gates carved with divine script in ancient elvish whisper in languages that predate mortal speech.

The city was lost to memory ages ago, its very existence forgotten until Lord Blackthorne's obsessive excavations began to uncover its outer chambers. The lord's journals, found scattered throughout the manor, reveal his growing fascination with the "impossible architecture" and "elvish inscriptions of divine power" he discovered in the depths. In his final entries, he refers to it only as "the City of the Gods" - a title that now sends chills through occult scholars who recognize its significance.

### Key Locations

**The Sundered Throne Hall**: Where the Eight once held council, now a vast chamber split between past and present. Ancient frescoes shift between showing divine glory and empty stone.

**The Whispering Gates**: Portals that once connected to the divine realms, now sealed but still echoing with otherworldly voices.

**The Eternal Library**: Astrael's great collection, where books exist in multiple time states - some readable only in the past, others only in the present.

**The Garden of Echoes**: Sylvara's domain, where phantom plants bloom in the spaces between moments.

**The Deep Vaults**: Khorvyn's workshops, now flooded and home to strange crystalline formations.

## 🐙 The Dimensional Gate

### The Final Secret

In the deepest vaults of the city lies a **dimensional gate** - a portal to a prison dimension where an ancient Aboleth is contained. This gate was created by the Lesser Gods themselves as a failsafe, a way to banish entities too dangerous for the Material Plane.

The Aboleth, known only as **The Whisperer in Depths**, possesses knowledge from before the divine age - secrets of true immortality, the nature of reality, and power beyond mortal comprehension. It was imprisoned here not by conquest, but by necessity, as its very presence threatened to unravel the foundations of existence.

### The Corruption

The gate's proximity to the Material Plane has slowly leaked alien influence into the surrounding area. This corruption manifested first in the city's temporal fracture, then gradually spread upward through the stone. Lord Blackthorne's excavations brought him into contact with this influence, slowly transforming him as he delved deeper into the city's secrets.

The Aboleth's whispers can be heard by those who venture too deep, promising knowledge and power to any who would free it from its dimensional prison.

## 📚 Artifacts & Mysteries

### The Crown of Eight Aspects

A golden circlet bearing symbols of all Eight Lesser Gods, said to grant the wearer divine insight into the city's true nature.

### The Codex of Temporal Paths

A tome that shifts between past and present, containing maps of the city in both states.

### The Shard of Whispered Truths

A crystalline fragment that shows visions of the city's golden age, but slowly drives the viewer mad with impossible nostalgia and the Aboleth's alien whispers.

## 🗺️ Navigating the City

### The Beacon Stones

Ancient markers that pulse with divine light, helping travelers navigate between temporal states.

### The Singing Crystals

Khorvyn's creations that resonate with specific divine frequencies, allowing passage through certain sealed areas.

### The Prophet's Mirror

A silver disc that shows the "true" path through the city's shifting passages.

## 🧠 The Forgotten Truth

### A Lost Legacy

Aran'keth exists now only in fragments - ancient elvish texts that few can read, half-remembered myths that scholars dismiss as fantasy, and the fevered journals of a corrupted lord who delved too deep. Its very existence has been erased from common knowledge, surviving only in the deepest archives and the whispered warnings of those who understand the true dangers that lie beneath the world.

### The Sealed Knowledge

Lord Blackthorne's discovery died with him, his transformation serving as a warning to any who might follow. The manor's undercroft contains evidence of his findings, but the true depths remain sealed by divine will and eldritch corruption alike.

The city waits in darkness, its twin towers reaching toward stone sky, its gates still whispering in ancient elvish to halls that echo with the footsteps of forgotten gods.
