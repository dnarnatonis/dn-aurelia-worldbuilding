# North Alba: The Frosted Marches of the Silver Host

> “Where the wind meets the marsh, and the marsh meets the moonlight — there begins the patience of the North.”  
> — *Codex Basilikai*, Vol. IX: The Outer Provinces

---

## 🌍 Overview

North Alba stands as the northern frontier of the Silver Host, defined by misty borderlands, lingering autonomy, and a legacy of old sea-kings. Absorbed into the Empire under Basilissa Heliane II, its people retain stubborn traditions—part loyalist, part heretic—molded by wind, brine, and ancestral beliefs.

**Geographic Parallels:**  

- *Real-World Analogue:* North Frisia blended with Shetland  
- *Latitude:* ~58–60 °N  
- *Biome:* Cold maritime moor, salt-marsh, coastal heath  
- *Defining Feature:* The brooding Sea of the North (gales, rolling fogs)

## 🏞️ Landscape & Ecology

- **Meres & Fens:** Winding wetlands with sedge, sphagnum, salt grasses
- **High Moors:** Peat plateaus with dwarf birch and heather
- **Fauna:** Marsh geese, sea otters, gray wolves, mysterious “mere-whales”

**Natural Rhythms:**  

- *Flood Moons* (every 40 days): Highest tides, mark fishing cycles  
- *Storm Cycles*: Peak between Skiabloom and Thyellastephan (late autumn–midwinter)

---

## 🧭 Society & Culture

### Ethos

Seclusion, stoicism, and reverence for nature’s cycles; Albans guard the border between Sea and Veil.

### Social Structure

- **Mere-houses:** Ancestral lines, rooted in earliest settlers and peat-cutters
- **Salt Barons:** Merchant elites controlling brine flats & salt trade
- **Storm-Wardens:** Coastal guardians maintaining dikes, lighthouses
- **Mere-wardens:** Marsh guides—part legend, part necessity

### Religion

- *Dual observance:* Imperial Luminism + ancient Alban animism  
- Host priests adopt lunar/algal timing; shrines often double as tidal markers  
- *Notable site:* Pyrgos Selenis (Duskport)—famed for aligning moonrise with its crystal dome

### Key Festivals (Imperial-aligned)

- **Heliaka Mysteria** (*Theogennetos*): Sun-renewal, midsummer
- **Mysteria Lunaris** (*Selenweave*): Women's night, Sea Mother offerings
- **Skotiomysteria** (*Nyxweave*): Dark solstice, renewal bonfires on frozen piers
- **Mere-Feast** (*Sophiatide*): Autumn harvest, gathering of noble houses

## 🧶 Material Culture

- **Textiles:**  
  - *Alba-weave*: Salt-resistant wool with complex geometric patterns  
  - *Storm-knots*: Intricate knotwork used in clothing and decorations  
  - *Marsh-lace*: Delicate reed-fiber lacework depicting local flora and fauna, used for ritual veils
- **Crafts:**  
  - *Peat-carving*: Decorative charms and reliquaries carved from dried peat  
  - *Salt-patterns*: Ephemeral art created during salt harvesting (Kalymfall drying flats)  
  - *Weather-stones*: Carved stones marking significant storms or events
- **Music:**  
  - *Mere-pipes*: Reed instruments made from marsh plants  
  - *Drift-drums*: Percussion instruments for storm songs  
  - *Telling-circles*: Formal storytelling gatherings around the hearth, preserving oral history of lost villages

---

## ⚔️ Political & Military Notes

- **Governance:** Semi-autonomous under the Legate of the Northern Marches
- **Defenses:** Local militias and coastal forts (Order of the Silver Wave)
- **Loyalty:** Practical, not zealous; tribute in salt is typical

**Ongoing Tensions:**  

- Skirmishes with marsh-bandits  
- Growing superstitions and unrest linked to corruption in the Withered Thicket

## 🕯️ Imperial Integration

North Alba tracks the *Imerologion Basilikon*, but interprets seasons through local symbolism:

| Imperial Cycle | Local Term      | Local Significance                        |
|:--------------:|:---------------|:-------------------------------------------|
| Aurorae        | Thawing Cycle  | Renewal, trade routes open                 |
| Zenith         | Highlight      | Fishing peaks, wool fairs                  |
| Hesperis       | Fading Sun     | Harvest, ancestor remembrances             |
| Kalymma        | Veiled Time    | Storm season, self-reflection, salt rites  |

*Regional Quirk:* The civic year often starts at **Sophiatide** (the harvest moon), not Basilisdawn—a subtle, enduring act of cultural independence.

## 📖 Field Notes for Storytellers

**Tone:** Austere, melancholic; stoic beauty  
**Colors:** Slate gray, moss green, salt white, tarnished gold  
**Themes:** Survival, heritage, faith vs. pragmatism, memory of the sea

### Plot Hooks

- A Mere-house heir vanishes during the Storm-vigil.
- Peat-cutters uncover a pre-Host shrine oozing black salt.
- The Pyrgos Selenis of Duskport predicts a "tide without moon."
- Salt Barons feud over control of the Withered Thicket's corrupted springs.
