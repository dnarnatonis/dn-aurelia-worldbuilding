# Wolfhaven

A frontier village in the Westmark Hills, where the Great Forest meets settled lands. Originally a hunting camp, now serves as a vital stop for traders and travelers.

## Overview

**Population**: ~150 permanent residents  
**Government**: Led by Thane Gerhard with village council  
**Defenses**: Wooden palisade, militia, natural barriers  

## Notable Features

### Location & Layout

- Built in defensible valley position
- Surrounded by forest and hills
- Single main road through village
- Central mead-hall as gathering point

### Economy

- Hunting and trapping
- Forest goods trading
- Basic crafting
- Traveler services

### Daily Life

- Most villagers hunt or gather
- Small-scale farming
- Trading with travelers
- Forest edge monitoring

## Current Situation

### Immediate Concerns

- Winter preparations
- Forest predators
- Bandit activity
- Supply shortages

### Ongoing Issues

- Monster sightings increasing
- Trade route dangers
- Resource management
- Outside pressure

## Notable Locations

### The Mead-Hall

- **Wolfhaven Hall**: Central gathering place and chief's residence
  - Built on the foundations of an ancient hill fort
  - Serves as meeting hall, feast house, and emergency shelter
  - Run by Thane Gerhard, a respected warrior-turned-chieftain

### Commerce

- **The Trading Square**
  - Open market space surrounded by permanent stalls
  - Busy during monthly market days
  - Meeting point for tribal traders and imperial merchants

- **Hildegard's Storehouse**
  - Main trading post for supplies and equipment
  - Buys furs, amber, and forest goods from hunters
  - Run by Hildegard, a shrewd merchant with imperial connections

### Services

- **The Forge**
  - Workshop of Gunther the Smith
  - Repairs weapons and tools
  - Learned metalworking from the dwarves

- **The Huntsman's Rest**
  - Inn and tavern for travelers
  - Run by the one-eyed veteran Erik and his family
  - Popular gathering spot for local hunters and guides

### Sacred Sites

- **The Grove**
  - Ancient sacred grove just outside the village
  - Site of religious ceremonies and tribal gatherings
  - Tended by Wulfhild, the elderly keeper of old ways

## Notable Figures

### Leadership

- **Thane Gerhard**: Village chief and warrior
  - Former tribal champion
  - Maintains peace between different groups
  - Seeks to preserve independence while working with empire

- **Folkmar**: Thing-speaker and lore keeper
  - Knows ancient laws and customs
  - Mediates disputes
  - Suspicious of imperial influence

### Other Notable Residents

- **Hildegard**: Merchant
  - Has contacts throughout Stonewood
  - Knows valuable trade secrets
  - Source of news and rumors

- **Sigrid**: Master Hunter
  - Leads hunting parties
  - Knows the surrounding wilderness
  - Trains young hunters

## Threats and Hooks

### Immediate Area

- Wild beasts in nearby forests
- Bandits targeting trade caravans
- Territorial disputes with neighboring tribes
- Strange ruins in the hills

### Ongoing Issues

- Growing imperial influence
- Dwarven prospectors seeking mining rights
- Ancient burial grounds disturbed by settlers
- Missing hunters and travelers

## Adventure Hooks

1. **The Missing Caravan**
   - Merchant convoy disappeared on the old forest road
   - Could be bandits, beasts, or worse

2. **Dwarven Diplomacy**
   - Dwarven delegation arriving to negotiate mining rights
   - Risk of offending either dwarves or tribal elders

3. **The Old Barrow**
   - Ancient burial mound discovered during construction
   - Contains both treasures and dangers

4. **Border Troubles**
   - Neighboring tribe claims hunting grounds
   - Need to negotiate or risk blood feud

5. **Imperial Interest**
   - Imperial surveyor arriving to map the region
   - Locals fear this precedes greater imperial control

## Campaign Use

Wolfhaven serves as an excellent starting point for new adventurers:

- Small enough to be manageable
- Mix of cultures provides varied interactions
- Nearby wilderness for exploration
- Local tensions create natural conflicts
- Connection to larger regional politics
- Easy access to both civilized and wild areas

The village can be used to introduce players to:

- Alaman tribal culture
- Imperial-tribal relations
- Dwarven diplomacy
- Frontier life
- Ancient mysteries
