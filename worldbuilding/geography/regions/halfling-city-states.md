# **The Halfling City-States**  

The **Halflings of the Southern Coast** are divided between **two city-states**, **Bayshire** and **Saltmere**, each representing a different way of life.

## **A. Bayshire – The Mercantile Hub**  

- **A thriving port city**, built on **trade, banking, and agriculture**.  
- **The wealthiest halfling settlement**, controlling land-based commerce and **imperial trade routes.**  
- **Ruled by a Lord Mayor** and a **Merchant-Prince Council**—a mix of aristocrats, bankers, and shipping magnates.  
- **Strong ties to the Empire**—many of its merchants have imperial connections, ensuring protection and influence.  
- **Surrounded by vast farms and vineyards**, feeding both local markets and export trade.  

## **B. Saltmere – The Seafaring Stronghold**  

- **A rugged coastal city**, known for **whaling, shipbuilding, and exploration.**  
- **Famous for its daring sailors, privateers, and deep-sea fishing industries.**  
- **Ruled by the Sea Speaker**, a leader chosen from among the most experienced captains.  
- **Less bureaucratic than Bayshire, more focused on survival, independence, and honor.**  
- **A naval power in its own right**, often at odds with imperial interests but too valuable to subdue.  

---

## **Cultural Divide: Two Halfling Ways of Life**  

### **A. City & Farm-Dwelling Halflings (Bayshire & Surrounding Lands)**  

- **Pragmatic, wealth-focused, and rooted in stability.**  
- **Control trade, banking, and land-based agriculture.**  
- **Excel in commerce, governance, and law**, ensuring their economic dominance.  
- **View their seafaring kin as reckless adventurers.**  
- Prefer **diplomacy, contracts, and calculated deals** over direct conflict.  

### **B. Seafaring Halflings (Saltmere & The Outer Isles)**  

- **Free-spirited, adventurous, and fiercely independent.**  
- **Live by the tides, thriving as sailors, traders, and privateers.**  
- **Masters of navigation, shipbuilding, and naval combat.**  
- **Distrust centralized power**, seeing imperial bureaucracy as restrictive.  
- **Regard their land-dwelling kin as too cautious and obsessed with gold.**  

---

## **Political Status & Relationship with the Empire**  

- **The Halfling City-States are semi-independent**, granted an **imperial charter in exchange for naval and economic contributions.**  
- **Bayshire works closely with imperial officials**, ensuring mutual prosperity.  
- **Saltmere remains wary of imperial control**, resisting direct taxation and military conscription.  
- **Both cities contribute gold and ships** but refuse to send land-based troops to imperial wars.  
- **Pirates, rival merchants, and foreign threats challenge their independence**, forcing them to walk a fine line between **loyalty and self-rule.**  

---

## **4. External & Internal Conflicts**  

1. **Piracy & Privateering** – The **Sea of the North** is full of opportunistic raiders. **Saltmere halflings hunt them, but some blur the line between defense and piracy.**  
2. **Imperial Pressure** – The **Empire wants more control over halfling trade and military power, but the city-states resist.**  
3. **Merchant Wars** – **Bayshire’s noble merchants clash with foreign traders and even imperial nobles, vying for economic dominance.**  
4. **Smuggling & The Black Market** – **Saltmere is a key smuggling hub**, where **The Veil (Thieves' Guild)** and other factions move contraband.
