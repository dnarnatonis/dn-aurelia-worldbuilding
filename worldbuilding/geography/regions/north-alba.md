# North Alba

North Alba represents the northernmost reaches of the Silver Host, a harsh and windswept region reminiscent of the North Frisian coastline. This untamed frontier marks where the Host's theocratic influence begins to fade into the wild unknown.

## Geography & Climate

- **Coastline**: Rugged, storm-battered shores facing the Sea of the North
- **Wetlands**: Vast networks of marshes and bogs, locally called "meres"
- **Inland**: Rolling hills covered in hardy grasslands and sparse woodlands
- **Notable Features**:
  - The Withered Thicket (corrupted forest around Black Hollow, tainted by the Hag of the Corrupted Forest's influence)
  - The Mere-ways (ancient paths through treacherous marshlands)
  - The Storm Cliffs (towering coastal barriers)

## Culture & People

The North Albans are a hardy folk, shaped by their harsh environment and relative isolation from the Host's heartland. Their culture reflects both their Frisian-like heritage and the necessities of frontier life, with a complex relationship to the Host's strict religious doctrine.

### Cultural Traits

- **Self-Reliance**: Communities must be largely self-sufficient
- **Oral Traditions**: Strong storytelling culture, particularly about local dangers
- **Superstition**: Deep respect for old warnings and folklore, sometimes at odds with official doctrine
- **Pragmatism**: Resources are precious; waste is considered a grave sin

### Settlements

- **Duskport**: Largest settlement, primary trading port
- **Black Hollow**: Frontier hamlet, struggling against supernatural forces
- **Scattered Steadings**: Small, fortified farming communities
- **Fishing Villages**: Dotting the coastline, often temporary due to storms

## Economy & Trade

### Primary Resources

- **Peat**: Cut from the meres, used for fuel
- **Fish**: Despite dangerous waters, fishing remains crucial
- **Wool**: Hardy sheep graze the inland hills
- **Salt**: Harvested from coastal flats

### Trade Relations

- **Maritime Trade**: Limited due to treacherous waters
- **Host Commerce**: Sporadic caravans via the Duskport road
- **Local Exchange**: Strong bartering system between communities
- **Border Trade**: Cautious commerce with imperial territories

## Threats & Challenges

### Natural Dangers

- **Winter Storms**: Brutal gales from the Sea of the North
- **Treacherous Meres**: Shifting paths through deadly marshlands
- **Coastal Erosion**: Constantly threatening shoreline settlements

### Supernatural Threats

- **The Withered Thicket**: Source of corruption near Black Hollow
- **Mere-wraiths**: Spirits haunting the marshlands
- **Ancient Ruins**: Remnants of pre-Host settlements, often cursed

## Relations with the Silver Host

- **Governance**: Minimal direct theocratic oversight
- **Military**: Relies on local militia and religious knights
- **Taxation**: Irregular collection due to isolation
- **Faith**: Traditional beliefs blend with official doctrine

## Notable Figures

- **The Mere-wardens**: Guides who navigate the dangerous wetlands
- **Storm Readers**: Respected elders who predict weather patterns
- **The Salt Barons**: Wealthy families controlling the salt trade
- **Frontier Priests**: Representatives of the Host's faith

## Future Prospects

- **Development Potential**: Rich in resources but difficult to exploit
- **Strategic Value**: Buffer between the Host and northern threats
- **Cultural Tension**: Growing divide between local traditions and Host doctrine
