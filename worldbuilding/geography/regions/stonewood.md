# The Duchy of Stonewood

Stonewood stands as one of the Five Major Ducal Regions of the Aurelian Empire, a vast frontier territory of dense forests and rugged hills. While nominally under imperial control, the region maintains significant autonomy under its Alaman tribes, whose fierce independence and martial traditions often challenge imperial authority.

## Geography

### Landscape

- **The Great Forest**: Dense woodland covering most of the duchy, broken by scattered clearings and settlements
- **The Westmark Hills**: Rugged highlands forming a natural border with the Dwarven Clans
- **The Wolfspine Mountains**: Lesser mountain range splitting the region north to south
- **The Amber Rivers**: Network of swift-flowing rivers vital for trade and transport
- **Tribal Territories**: Traditional lands claimed by various Alaman tribes

### Notable Features

- **Hill Forts**: Ancient defensive positions atop strategic heights
- **The Waldmark**: Disputed borderlands between imperial territories and tribal regions
- **Old Roads**: Ancient stone roads, some dating to pre-imperial times
- **Sacred Groves**: Traditional gathering places for tribal assemblies

## Political Structure

### Governance

- Ruled through a complex web of tribal chieftains, noble houses, and imperial officials
- Power balanced between:
  - Tribal **Thingmen** (Free warriors with voting rights)
  - Noble **Waldhauser** (Prominent families)
  - The **Wald-thing** (Traditional assembly of free men)
- Imperial authority mainly limited to major trade routes and cities

### Major Settlements

- **Waldenhof**: The ducal seat, a fortified hill-town where the Thing meets
- **Bernmark**: Trading post at the intersection of tribal and dwarven territories
- **Eisenholz**: Imperial fortress-town guarding the eastern road
- **Wolfmark**: Traditional gathering place of the northern tribes

## Economy

### Resources

- **Timber**: Lumber from the vast forests
- **Amber**: Gathered along river valleys
- **Furs**: Hunting and trapping
- **Iron**: Small-scale mining in the hills
- **Cattle**: Raised in forest clearings and hill pastures

### Trade

- Export of timber, furs, and amber to the empire
- Trade with Dwarven Clans for metalwork and weapons
- Famous for leather working and fur preparation
- Local markets controlled by tribal custom rather than imperial law

## Military

### Forces

- **Tribal Warriors**: Each free man serves in their tribe's warband
- **Forest Scouts**: Light infantry skilled in woodland warfare
- **Noble Cavalry**: Mounted warriors from prominent families
- **Levy Spearmen**: Called up for defense or major campaigns

### Defenses

- Network of hill forts and wooden strongholds
- Tribal warning systems using horn calls and signal fires
- Strategic use of forest paths and ambush points
- Earthwork fortifications around major settlements

## Cultural Significance

### Imperial Relations

- Serves as a buffer between the empire and external threats
- Provides military auxiliaries for imperial campaigns
- Maintains traditional laws alongside imperial code
- Frequent source of border disputes and tribal raids

### Challenges

- Resistance to imperial taxation and authority
- Blood feuds between tribes and noble houses
- Protection of tribal sovereignty
- Balance between tribal custom and imperial law

## Current Issues

### Political Tensions

- Growing imperial presence in eastern regions
- Disputes over hunting and grazing rights
- Inter-tribal conflicts over territory
- Resistance to imperial religious conversion efforts

### Social Concerns

- Preservation of tribal traditions
- Integration of tribal and imperial justice
- Rights of free tribesmen versus imperial subjects
- Maintenance of traditional assembly powers

## Notable Features

### Traditional Practices

- Blood-price system for settling disputes
- Tribal election of chiefs and representatives
- Seasonal gatherings for trade and justice
- Warrior initiation rites

### Cultural Landmarks

- The Great Thing-stone: Traditional assembly place
- Tribal Burial Mounds: Ancient markers of territory
- Border Stones: Traditional boundary markers
- The Old Groves: Sacred assembly sites
