# The Thirsting Codex

Also known as:

- **High Elvish**: *Ithilnorith* ("Moon's Truth")
- **Abyssal**: *Vharzakos* ("Whispers of Madness")
- **Infernal**: *Morkhaz'thul* ("Tome of Corrupted Wisdom")

## True Origins

While many attribute the Book's creation to Maltherion, its true origins lie in the darkest period of High Elven history. During the reign of Queen Elathis Araenor, a secretive circle of High Elven archivists known as the *Ithilthar'ai* (Moon's Chosen) sought to document all forbidden knowledge in service to Ulmathar. Their work, however, drew the attention of Tharizkorr, the Old God of Rot, whose corrupting essence began seeping into their sacred texts from its prison in the Rotting Hollow.

The book began as the *Ithilnorith* (Moon's Truth), but as each archivist added their discoveries, Tharizkorr's corruption grew stronger. The rot eventually drove the Ithilthar'ai mad, their final entries becoming increasingly disturbing until they vanished entirely, leaving only the Book behind.

Shalzharae, the Mother of Lies, later discovered the Book and, recognizing its potential, wove her deceptions through its rotting pages. Through her manipulations, she guided the Book into the hands of Maltherion, Lord of the Hells, who added his own infernal knowledge to its contents. Through this unholy trinity of corruption—Tharizkorr's rot, Shalzharae's lies, and Maltherion's infernal power—the Book gained its current reputation as a source of forbidden knowledge and corrupting power.

## Random Properties

### Minor Beneficial Properties

1. **Sentinel of Secrets**: The book glows faintly when mind flayers or other aberrations are within 120 feet.
2. **Guardian of Knowledge**: The bearer gains a +2 bonus to Initiative rolls if not incapacitated.
3. **Strange Material**: The book's pages are made from an unknown material that feels like living shadow, yet can be touched and turned like normal paper.

### Major Beneficial Property

**Keeper of Forbidden Lore**: Once per dawn, you can ask the book a question about any evil entity, artifact, or event. Roll a d20. On a 15 or higher, the book reveals the answer through a series of shifting images and whispered words in ancient languages.

### Minor Detrimental Properties

1. **Covetous Nature**: You become obsessed with hoarding knowledge, especially forbidden secrets.
2. **Painful Insight**: You experience sharp headaches whenever you read from the book.
3. **Repulsive Aura**: Small animals flee from your presence, and plants wither slightly when you handle the book.

### Major Detrimental Properties

1. **Corrupting Influence**: Each time you spend more than an hour reading the book, you must make a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, you gain one level of exhaustion that can't be removed until you spend a day not carrying the book.
2. **Shadow's Mark**: Your shadow sometimes moves independently of you, especially in moonlight, and appears to reach for nearby creatures.

## Thematic Elements

The book's corruption manifests differently based on its reader's connection to various powers:

- **High Elf Readers**: Experience visions of Ulmathar's moon and hear whispers in ancient Elvish
- **Divine Spellcasters**: Feel the weight of Tharizkorr's rot trying to corrupt their divine connection
- **Arcane Spellcasters**: Sense the book attempting to twist their magical knowledge toward darker purposes

### Spells

While attuned to the book and holding it, you can cast the following spells (save DC 18) from it:

- *Animate Dead* (Tharizkorr's rot giving false life)
- *Circle of Death* (Maltherion's infernal power)
- *Dominate Monster* (Shalzharae's manipulation)
- *Finger of Death* (The combined corruption of all three powers)

Once you use the book to cast a spell, you can't cast that spell again from it until the next dawn.

### Dark Knowledge

You can reference the Ithilnorith whenever you make an Intelligence check to recall information about evil entities, artifacts, or events. When you do so, you have advantage on that check.

At the DM's discretion, the book might reveal forbidden secrets, such as:

- True names of powerful demons from Maltherion's Hells
- Rituals of rot from Tharizkorr's ancient teachings
- The Mother of Lies' methods of corruption
- Lost spells from the mad writings of the Ithilthar'ai

### Words of Corruption

While the book is on your person, you can take a Magic action to recite words from its pages in ancient High Elvish corrupted by Abyssal and Infernal influences. Each time you do so, you take 1d12 Psychic damage, and each creature within 15 feet of you takes 3d6 Psychic damage unless the creature is a Fiend or an Undead.

### Destroying the Book

The Ithilnorith allows pages to be torn from it, but any forbidden knowledge contained on those pages finds its way back into the book eventually, usually when a new scholar adds their own corrupted discoveries to the tome.

A creature attuned to the book for 100 years can discover a phrase hidden in the original High Elven text that, when translated to Abyssal and spoken aloud, destroys both the speaker and the book in a flash of purifying light. However, as long as the trinity of corruption—Tharizkorr's rot, Shalzharae's lies, and Maltherion's infernal power—exists in the world, the book reforms 1d10 × 100 years later.

### Lesser Versions

#### The Sealed Chronicle

The earliest form of the book appears as an ancient High Elven spellbook, its true nature sealed away by the last uncorrupted Ithilthar'ai. The text is written in a complex lunar cipher that can only be read under moonlight. Most readers assume it's merely an eccentric wizard's spellbook, though certain passages hint at deeper, darker knowledge.

**Properties:**

- Functions as a spellbook containing several common wizard spells
- Advantage on Intelligence checks related to lunar magic and High Elven history
- The bearer experiences unusually vivid dreams during the full moon
- **Minor Signs**: Small animals grow uneasy around the book, and the pages feel unusually cold to the touch
- **Hidden Nature**: A successful DC 20 Arcana check reveals traces of ancient wards containing something within the text
