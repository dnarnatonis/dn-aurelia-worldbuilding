Abyssal is an ancient language deeply rooted in the study of primordial forces. Based on archaeological evidence, it was primarily used in religious and magical contexts, particularly in rituals concerning the fundamental forces of existence: Chaos (Kaziru), Creation (Banuātu), and Destruction (Halqu).

## Phonological System

### Consonants

The language features a rich consonantal system, particularly notable for its guttural sounds:

- Stops: k, g, q, t, d, p, b
- Fricatives: z, s, š (sh), ṣ, h
- Liquids and Nasals: l, r, m, n
- Other: ʔ (glottal stop)

### Vowels

Abyssal distinguishes between short and long vowels:

- Short: a, e, i, u
- Long: ā, ē, ī, ū (marked by doubling)

## Morphological Structure

### Root System

The foundation of Abyssal is its triconsonantal root system. Each root carries a basic meaning that can be modified through various patterns:

Example using K-Z-R (chaos):

- kazru (chaos)
- kuzur (chaotic being)
- kazrātu (chaotic forces)

### Grammatical Features

- **Plurality**:
  - Masculine plural: -ū
  - Feminine plural: -āt

- **Possession**:
  - First person: -ī (my)
  - Second person: -ka (your)
  - Third person: -šu (his)

## Syntax

Abyssal follows a Verb-Subject-Object (VSO) word order, placing emphasis on actions rather than actors. This reflects the language's focus on processes and transformations rather than static states.

### Tense System

The language employs a prefix system to mark tense and aspect:

- Completed actions: iprus ("he cut off")
- Ongoing actions: iparras ("he is cutting off")

## Sacred Terminology

### Fundamental Forces

1. **Kaziru** (Chaos)
   - Root: K-Z-R
   - Etymology: "That which is scattered"
   - Usage: Often appears in texts describing the primordial void

2. **Banuātu** (Creation)
   - Root: B-N-ʔ
   - Etymology: "The one who builds"
   - Grammatical note: Feminine form

3. **Halqu** (Destruction)
   - Root: H-L-Q
   - Etymology: "The perishing force"
   - Grammatical note: Masculine form

4. **Ṣimahhu** (Nature)
   - Root: Ṣ-M-H
   - Etymology: "The flourishing force"
   - Alternative form: Ṣimvirid (hybrid with Sylvan influence)

Abyssal script is a modified form of cuneiform, adapted to represent its unique phonological system. The writing system consists of:

1. **Root Symbols**: Basic shapes representing triconsonantal roots
2. **Diacritical Marks**: Indicating vowel sounds
3. **Divine Logograms**: Special symbols for cosmic concepts
