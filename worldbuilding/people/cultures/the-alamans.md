# Alaman Culture

The Alamans of Stonewood maintain proud traditions that emphasize self-reliance, craftsmanship, and harmony with the forest. Their culture stands distinct from both the Empire's refinement and the Dwarven Clans' mountain traditions.

## Cuisine

### Staple Foods

- **Forest Bread**: Dark bread made with foraged nuts and berries
- **Smoke-dried Venison**: Preserved game meat with pine smoke
- **Wild Mushroom Stew**: Hearty stew with foraged fungi
- **Forest-honey Porridge**: Grain porridge sweetened with wild honey
- **Root Cakes**: Dense cakes made from forest tubers

### Notable Dishes

- **Hunter's Feast**: Communal dish of multiple game meats and forest herbs
- **Tree-sap Glazed Fish**: River fish preserved with pine sap
- **Forest-floor Soup**: Rich soup made with layers of forest ingredients
- **Woodcutter's Pie**: Savory pie filled with game and wild vegetables

### Drinking Culture

- **Pine Beer**: Distinctive beer brewed with pine needles
- **Berry Wine**: Strong wine made from forest berries
- **Root Tea**: Warming tea made from medicinal roots
- **Tree-sap Mead**: Honey mead infused with tree sap

## Arts & Crafts

### Woodworking

- **Living Wood**: Furniture carved from still-living trees
- **Bark-craft**: Decorative items made from various tree barks
- **Forest-knots**: Intricate wooden joints without metal

### Textile Arts

- **Forest-weave**: Cloth combining wool with tree fibers
- **Bark-cloth**: Soft fabric made from processed tree bark
- **Beast-thread**: Textiles incorporating animal hair with wool

### Visual Arts

- **Tree-marking**: Symbolic carvings that tell stories
- **Root-patterns**: Designs inspired by tree root systems
- **Beast-masks**: Ceremonial masks depicting forest creatures

## Naming Conventions

### Given Names

- **Male**: Adalbert, Bernhard, Friedrich, Gerhard, Ludwig, Siegfried, Wolfram, Theobald
- **Female**: Adelheid, Brunhild, Ermengard, Gertrud, Hildegard, Mathilde, Waldburg, Sieglinde

### Family Names

- **Geographic**: Waldmann, Forster, Holzmann, Bergmann
- **Occupational**: Jäger, Zimmermann, Köhler, Gerber
- **Descriptive**: Wolfhart, Eichmann, Tannenmann
- **Patronymic**: Friedrichsohn, Bernhardling

### Naming Traditions

- Children receive a birth-name and earn a forest-name upon proving themselves
- Family names often incorporate natural elements
- Use of "von" to denote connection to specific forest territories

## Noble Practices

### Noble Houses

- **Structure**: Organized into "Waldhauser" (Forest Houses)
- **Titles**:
  - Waldgraf/Waldgräfin (Forest Count/Countess)
  - Jagdmeister/Jagdmeisterin (Hunt Master/Mistress)
  - Holzherr/Holzfrau (Wood Lord/Lady)

### Noble Traditions

- **Forest-moot**: Seasonal gathering of noble houses
- **Tree-binding**: Ritual where new nobles plant their house tree
- **Beast-vigil**: Night spent alone in the deep forest
- **Wood-right**: Ceremonial passing of family axe to heirs

### Political Customs

- **Wald-thing**: Assembly of forest nobles and free folk
- **Hunt-council**: Strategic meetings during major hunts
- **Wood-peace**: Traditional laws governing forest use
- **Border-walking**: Ritual marking of territory boundaries

## Religious Practices

### Forest Traditions

- **Tree-speaking**: Communion with ancient trees
- **Beast-blessing**: Rituals honoring hunted animals
- **Wood-warding**: Protection ceremonies for forests

### Integration with Imperial Faith

- Blend of forest spirits and imperial saints
- Sacred groves as unofficial shrines
- Seasonal festivals combining both traditions

## Social Customs

### Hospitality

- **Hearth-sharing**: Sacred duty to shelter travelers
- **Hunt-feast**: Sharing of successful hunts
- **Wood-welcome**: Offering of carved tokens to guests

### Coming of Age

- **Forest-walking**: Solo survival in deep woods
- **Beast-tracking**: First independent hunt
- **Tree-choosing**: Selection of personal tree spirit

### Marriage Customs

- **Grove-binding**: Ceremonies in sacred forest clearings
- **Wood-weaving**: Exchange of carved symbols
- **Beast-calling**: Ritual seeking animal omens for union

## Crafting Traditions

### Wood Crafting Traditions

- Emphasis on working with living wood
- Techniques for preserving tree life while harvesting
- Sacred geometries in construction

### Hunting

- Strict codes of sustainable hunting
- Rituals for honoring taken game
- Traditional tracking methods

### Forest Management

- Sustainable forestry practices
- Sacred grove preservation
- Living boundary markers
