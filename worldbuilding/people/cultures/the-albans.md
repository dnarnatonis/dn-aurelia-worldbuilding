# Alban Culture

The Albans maintain distinct cultural traditions that predate their incorporation into the Silver Host, blending ancient customs with newer religious observances.

## Cuisine

### Staple Foods

- **Peat-Smoke Fish**: Preserved fish smoked using local peat
- **Black Bread**: Dense, dark bread made from hardy northern grains
- **Mere-root Stew**: Hearty stew made with local tubers and wild herbs
- **Salt Mutton**: Preserved sheep meat, often served with root vegetables
- **Oatcakes**: Traditional flatbreads eaten at most meals

### Notable Dishes

- **Alban Pudding**: Black pudding made with sheep's blood and oats
- **Storm Soup**: Fish soup traditionally made during fierce weather
- **Marsh-berry Preserve**: Sweet-tart preserve made from local berries
- **Shepherd's Comfort**: Layered dish of mutton, roots, and oat topping

### Drinking Culture

- **Mere-beer**: Dark ale brewed with local water and herbs
- **Heather Wine**: Sweet wine made from highland heather honey
- **Storm Tea**: Strong, dark tea served with sheep's milk

## Arts & Crafts

### Textile Arts

- **Alba-weave**: Distinctive wool textile with complex geometric patterns
- **Storm-knots**: Intricate knotwork used in clothing and decorations
- **Marsh-lace**: Delicate lacework depicting local flora and fauna

### Music & Performance

- **Storm-songs**: Traditional ballads about weather and sea
- **Mere-pipes**: Reed instruments made from marsh plants
- **Telling-circles**: Formal storytelling gatherings around the hearth

### Visual Arts

- **Peat-carving**: Decorative objects carved from dried peat
- **Salt-patterns**: Temporary artwork created during salt harvesting
- **Weather-stones**: Carved stones marking significant storms or events

## Naming Conventions

### Given Names

- **Male**: Aldwin, Cedric, Dunstan, Egbert, Godric, Oswald, Thurstan, Wilfred
- **Female**: Audrey, Edith, Hilda, Mildred, Rowena, Sigrid, Winifred, Æthelflæd

### Family Names

- **Geographic**: Marshman, Merewood, Stormcliff, Deepmere
- **Occupational**: Shepherd, Fisher, Saltmaker, Peatcutter
- **Patronymic**: Alderson, Cedricson, Oswalding
- **Descriptive**: Blackthorne, Stormwind, Strongarm

### Naming Traditions

- Children often receive a "soul-name" at birth and a "true-name" upon coming of age
- Noble families maintain both an Alban name and a Host-approved religious name
- Use of "the" with descriptive surnames (e.g., "John the Stormwind")

## Noble Practices

### Noble Houses

- **Structure**: Organized into ancient "Mere-houses" predating the Host
- **Titles**:
  - Mere-lord/Mere-lady (local nobility)
  - Storm-warden (coastal defenders)
  - Salt-baron/Salt-baroness (trade nobility)

### Noble Traditions

- **The Mere-feast**: Annual gathering of noble houses
- **Storm-vigil**: Ritual where heirs spend a night facing the sea
- **Salt-binding**: Marriage tradition involving exchange of pure salt
- **Hearth-right**: Ceremonial passing of family hearth-fire to heirs

### Political Customs

- **Mere-council**: Regular meeting of local noble houses
- **Storm-moot**: Emergency gathering during crises
- **Salt-tithe**: Traditional exchange of salt between houses
- **Mere-peace**: Ancient custom of neutrality in sacred marsh-lands

### Noble Education

- Training in both Alban traditions and Host doctrine
- Emphasis on weather-reading and navigation
- Martial training focused on defensive tactics
- Diplomatic education balancing local and Host interests

## Religious Practices

### Traditional Beliefs

- **Weather-watching**: Reading natural omens
- **Mere-blessing**: Rituals for safe passage through marshlands
- **Storm-speaking**: Ancient weather-influencing ceremonies

### Integration with Host Faith

- Official Host ceremonies incorporating local traditions
- Dual observance of religious festivals
- Careful balance between old ways and new doctrine

## Social Customs

### Hospitality

- **Hearth-right**: Sacred obligation to shelter travelers
- **Storm-sharing**: Communal shelter during harsh weather
- **Salt-blessing**: Offering salt to guests

### Coming of Age

- **Mere-walking**: Solo navigation of marsh paths
- **Storm-standing**: Ritual facing of severe weather
- **Salt-gathering**: First independent salt harvest

### Marriage Customs

- **Mere-binding**: Traditional handfasting with marsh reeds
- **Storm-promise**: Vows exchanged during rough weather
- **Salt-sharing**: Exchange of family salt stores
