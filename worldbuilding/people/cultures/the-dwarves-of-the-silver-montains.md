# Silver Mountain Dwarven Culture

The Dwarves of the Silver Mountains maintain ancient traditions centered on craftsmanship, ancestral reverence, and the sanctity of stone. Their culture is deeply influenced by their mountain homeland, rich mineral resources, and position between the Empire's eastern plains and the militant Silver Host.

## Cuisine

### Staple Foods

- **Stone Bread**: Dense, mineral-rich bread baked on hot stone
- **Cave Mushroom Stew**: Hearty stew from fungi cultivated in deep mines
- **Smoked Mountain Goat**: Preserved meat cured with rare mountain herbs
- **Mineral Broth**: Nutrient-rich soup containing trace metals and minerals
- **Root Paste**: Concentrated food made from mountain tubers

### Notable Dishes

- **Forge Feast**: Ceremonial meal prepared on smithing anvils during festivals
- **Gem-baked Fish**: River fish cooked encased in mineral salts
- **Mountain Core Pie**: Savory pie with layers representing mountain strata
- **Ancestors' Plate**: Mixed food offering containing symbolically important ingredients

### Drinking Culture

- **Silver Ale**: Strong beer brewed with water from mineral springs
- **Stone Liquor**: Potent spirit distilled through limestone filters
- **Deep Tea**: Dark, earthy tea made from cave-grown fungi
- **Blood of the Mountain**: Ruby-red wine aged in sealed mine chambers

## Arts & Crafts

### Metalworking

- **Silver-etching**: Intricate designs carved into precious metals
- **Rune-forging**: Embedding symbolic patterns into functional objects
- **Ancestor Masks**: Metal face coverings depicting honored forebears
- **Living Metal**: Alloys treated to change appearance over time

### Stone Arts

- **Echo Stone**: Resonant stone sculptures that carry sound
- **Vein Carving**: Following natural mineral veins in stone for artistic effect
- **Memory Pillars**: Record-keeping columns chronicling clan histories
- **Pressure Gems**: Crystals formed through controlled pressure techniques

### Visual Arts

- **Depth Murals**: Multi-layered images revealing different scenes at various angles
- **Metal Weaving**: Fine metal threads interwoven to create tapestries
- **Runescapes**: Symbolic landscapes encoded with clan histories
- **Light Chambers**: Rooms designed to create specific light patterns

## Naming Conventions

### Given Names

- **Male**: Barendin, Durin, Gimli, Thrain, Thorin, Dwalin, Gloin, Nori
- **Female**: Dis, Thyra, Hilda, Dagmar, Brynja, Frida, Sigrun, Gudrun

### Family Names

- **Clan-based**: Ironbeard, Stonehammer, Silverfist, Deepforge
- **Occupation**: Anvilhand, Runecarver, Gempicker, Vaultguard
- **Lineage**: Durinsson, Thraindottir
- **Descriptive**: Stoutheart, Trueaxe, Hardstone

### Naming Traditions

- Dwarves receive a birth-name and earn a craft-name upon completing their masterwork
- Clan names take precedence in formal situations
- Use of ancestral names to honor important forebears
- Deep-names known only to the individual and their closest kin

## Noble Practices

### Noble Houses

- **Structure**: Organized into ancient clans led by Thanes
- **Titles**:
  - Thane/Thaness (Clan Leader)
  - Forge-Master/Forge-Mistress (Craft Leader)
  - Deep-Warden/Deep-Guardian (Defensive Leader)

### Noble Traditions

- **Anvil Council**: Gathering of clan leaders at forge-lit meetings
- **Stone Oath**: Ritual where promises are carved into living stone
- **Deep Descent**: Ceremonial journey to ancient halls for coronation
- **Forge Right**: Passing of ancestral hammer to new clan leaders

### Political Customs

- **Mountain Moot**: Assembly of all clans for major decisions
- **Craft Guilds**: Power structures separate from but equal to noble lines
- **Oath Stones**: Physical records of agreements between clans
- **Mine Rights**: Complex laws governing mineral claims and excavation

## Religious Practices

### Stone Traditions

- **Ancestor Vigil**: Communication with departed clan members
- **Rune Reading**: Divination through stone casting
- **Forge Blessing**: Rituals for sanctifying new metalwork
- **Stone Listening**: Meditation to hear the voice of the mountain

### Integration with Imperial Faith

- Respectful acknowledgment of Classical Gods, especially Khorvyn (Duragoth)
- Maintenance of separate ancestral practices
- Interpretation of Auriana as a distant but respected power
- Pragmatic adaptation of compatible rituals

## Social Customs

### Hospitality

- **Hearth Sharing**: Sacred obligation to provide warmth and shelter
- **Stone Meal**: Traditional offering of food to visitors
- **Mine Welcome**: Ritualized tour of clan holdings for honored guests

### Coming of Age

- **Deep Delving**: Solo journey into unmapped tunnels
- **First Forge**: Creation of personal tools and weapons
- **Ancestor Naming**: Ceremonial connection to specific forebear

### Marriage Customs

- **Stone Binding**: Exchange of personally crafted metal rings
- **Clan Merging**: Formal negotiations between families
- **Forge Union**: Joint creation of a symbolic item representing the bond

## Crafting Traditions

### Mining Techniques

- Emphasis on sustainable excavation
- Rituals for respecting the mountain's bounty
- Specialized tools passed through generations
- Mapping systems recording geological features

### Forging

- Family-specific alloy recipes kept secret
- Rune embellishments for practical and spiritual purposes
- Apprenticeship system requiring decades of training
- Connection between smith and material viewed as sacred

### Runecraft

- System of symbols containing historical and magical significance
- Techniques for embedding runes in different materials
- Belief that properly crafted runes channel ancestral power
- Guild of runepriests maintaining ancient knowledge

## Relations with Neighbors

### The Empire

- Trade relationships based on metal goods and craftsmanship
- Careful maintenance of independence while remaining allied
- Cultural exchange limited to practical knowledge
- Respect for imperial power tempered by historical perspective

### The Silver Host

- Complex relations with the breakaway kingdom
- Some clans provide weapons and armor to the militant theocracy
- Others maintain neutrality to protect mining interests
- Concern about religious zealotry affecting traditional trade routes

### Other Dwarven Clans

- Strong bonds with western Ironshield and Stonefist clans
- Competitive but respectful relations with mountain neighbors
- Regular exchange of craft techniques and resources
- United front when facing external threats

## Current Challenges

### Political Pressures

- Balancing relations between Empire and Silver Host
- Maintaining autonomy while benefiting from alliances
- Internal debates about isolationism versus engagement
- Protecting traditional clan territories

### Cultural Concerns

- Preservation of ancestral practices
- Integration of new techniques without losing tradition
- Maintaining population growth in limited mountain space
- Passing wisdom to younger generations increasingly drawn to imperial cities
