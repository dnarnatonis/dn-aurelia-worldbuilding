# The Wandering People (Ibaialdeko)

The Wandering People, known in their own language as the *Ibaialdeko* (River-Walkers), are a nomadic culture that has traveled the roads and waterways of the continent for over a millennium. Drawing from ancient traditions that predate the Aurelian Empire, they maintain a rich oral culture centered around storytelling, divination, and the preservation of pre-imperial wisdom.

Unlike the settled peoples of the Empire, the Wandering People view the world as a vast network of paths and possibilities, believing that movement itself is sacred and that staying in one place too long invites spiritual stagnation. They are renowned for their skills in fortune-telling, metalworking, horse-trading, and keeping the old stories alive.

## Cultural Structure & Organization

### The Family Clusters (*Etxaldeak*)

- **Extended Family Units**: Groups of 20-50 related individuals who travel together
- **Generational Leadership**: Elders make major decisions while adults handle daily operations
- **Specialization Roles**: Each family cluster develops expertise in specific trades or crafts
- **Marriage Networks**: Complex relationships between clusters that maintain genetic diversity and cultural unity

### The Seasonal Gatherings (*Urtaroak*)

- **Spring Convergence (*Udaberri-Biltzarra*)**: Annual gathering for marriages, trade, and storytelling competitions
- **Autumn Council (*Udazken-Kontseilua*)**: Meeting to share news, settle disputes, and plan winter routes
- **Sacred Observances**: Religious ceremonies that can only be performed when multiple clusters gather
- **Knowledge Exchange**: Sharing of new stories, songs, and magical techniques between families

### Social Hierarchy

1. **The Seers (*Ikusleak*)**: Individuals gifted with supernatural sight and mystical abilities
2. **The Elders (*Zaharrak*)**: Oldest members who serve as repositories of cultural memory
3. **The Storytellers (*Ipuin-Esaleak*)**: Keepers of oral tradition and cultural historians
4. **The Artisans (*Esku-Langilea*)**: Master craftspeople in traditional trades
5. **The Wanderers (*Ibiltzaileak*)**: Adult members who contribute to daily survival and travel

## Daily Life & Customs

### Nomadic Lifestyle

#### **Travel Patterns**

- **Seasonal Routes**: Well-established paths followed for generations, adapted to weather and local conditions
- **Sacred Sites**: Regular visits to ancient locations important to Wandering People spirituality
- **Trade Circuits**: Routes designed to maximize commercial opportunities with settled peoples
- **Weather Reading**: Deep knowledge of natural signs to predict safe travel conditions

#### **Living Arrangements**

- **Decorated Wagons**: Elaborately painted and carved mobile homes that display family history
- **Communal Spaces**: Shared areas for cooking, storytelling, and craft work
- **Sacred Objects**: Each family maintains a portable shrine for ancestor veneration
- **Adaptive Furnishing**: Furniture and tools designed for easy transport and multiple uses

### Traditional Occupations

#### **Performance Arts** *(Skilled Showmanship)*

- **Fortune Telling**: Expert psychological manipulation and cold reading using traditional props
- **Charm Making**: Crafting beautiful but non-magical amulets and decorative talismans  
- **"Curse Lifting"**: Theatrical rituals that provide psychological comfort and healing
- **Vision Interpretation**: Skillful interpretation of dreams using traditional folklore and psychology

*Note: While most Wandering People practice these as performance arts, rare individuals possess genuine mystical abilities.*

#### **Practical Trades**

- **Metalworking**: Exceptional skills in creating jewelry, tools, and decorative items
- **Horse Trading**: Breeding, training, and selling fine horses and other animals
- **Textile Arts**: Creating distinctive fabrics, embroidery, and decorative clothing
- **Entertainment**: Music, dance, and storytelling performances for settled communities

## Spiritual Beliefs & Practices

### Core Cosmology

#### **The Eternal Road (*Bide Betikoa*)**

- **Philosophical Foundation**: Belief that life itself is a journey with no final destination
- **Spiritual Movement**: Physical travel mirrors spiritual development and growth
- **Path Wisdom**: Different roads teach different lessons; variety of experience is essential
- **Sacred Geography**: Certain locations hold special spiritual significance for all Wandering People

#### **The Web of Stories (*Ipuin-Sarea*)**

- **Living History**: Belief that stories have power to shape reality and preserve truth
- **Collective Memory**: Cultural knowledge preserved through oral tradition rather than written records
- **Narrative Magic**: Storytelling itself is considered a form of sacred practice
- **Story Guardians**: Designated individuals responsible for preserving specific tales or knowledge

### Religious Practices

#### **Ancestor Veneration**

- **Spirit Roads**: Belief that deceased family members continue to travel spiritual paths
- **Memory Keeping**: Elaborate rituals for preserving the stories and wisdom of the dead
- **Guidance Seeking**: Consulting ancestor spirits for advice on important decisions
- **Sacred Objects**: Family heirlooms believed to carry the essence of departed relatives

#### **Traditional "Divination"** *(Mostly Performance)*

- **Card Reading**: Using decks of cards with symbolic meanings, combined with psychology and cold reading
- **Bone Casting**: Throwing marked bones in patterns that allow for skillful interpretation
- **Crystal Scrying**: Theatrical gazing into polished stones while making educated guesses
- **"Dream Walking"**: Psychological counseling disguised as mystical dream interpretation

*Note: Genuine seers exist but are extremely rare and often keep their true abilities hidden.*

#### **"Protective" Services** *(Comfort and Psychology)*

- **Charm Crafting**: Creating beautiful amulets that provide psychological comfort, not magical protection
- **"Circle Setting"**: Elaborate rituals that make people feel safer through ceremony and community
- **"Curse Detection"**: Identifying psychological or social problems and providing practical solutions
- **"Spiritual Cleansing"**: Therapeutic rituals that help people process trauma and negative experiences

## Arts & Cultural Expression

### Oral Tradition

#### **Epic Narratives**

- **The First Journey**: Creation myth describing how the Wandering People began their eternal travels
- **The Star-Walker's Tale**: Story of a legendary hero who traveled between worlds
- **The Lost Kingdom**: Tragic history of the Wandering People's ancient homeland
- **The Binding of Shadows**: Prophecy about the role of the Wandering People in preventing great darkness

#### **Moral Parables**

- **The Merchant and the Seer**: Teaching about the balance between material and spiritual concerns
- **The Road That Led Home**: Story about understanding that belonging comes from people, not places
- **The Three Crossroads**: Tale about making difficult choices when paths diverge
- **The Horse That Forgot How to Run**: Parable about losing essential nature through comfort

### Musical Arts

#### **Traditional Instruments**

- **Travel Harp (*Bideko-Arpa*)**: Portable harp used for storytelling accompaniment
- **Rhythm Bells (*Konpas-Kanpaina*)**: Worn while dancing to mark the beat of travel
- **Wind Pipes (*Haize-Txistua*)**: Flute-like instruments that mimic natural sounds
- **Drum Circle (*Danbor-Zirkulua*)**: Multiple drums used for ceremonial gatherings

#### **Musical Styles**

- **Journey Songs**: Traveling music designed to mark the rhythm of wagon wheels
- **Story Ballads**: Musical narratives that preserve cultural history
- **Fortune Melodies**: Songs used to enhance divination and mystical practices
- **Celebration Dances**: Energetic music for festivals and community gatherings

### Visual Arts

#### **Wagon Decoration**

- **Family Histories**: Painted scenes depicting important events in family genealogy
- **Protective Symbols**: Mystical designs believed to ward off dangers during travel
- **Geographic Markers**: Images representing significant places visited during travels
- **Seasonal Themes**: Decorations that change with the yearly cycle and celestial events

#### **Textile Arts**

- **Story Tapestries**: Woven narratives that can be "read" like illustrated books
- **Protective Clothing**: Garments embroidered with symbols that provide spiritual protection
- **Trade Goods**: Beautiful fabrics created specifically for sale to settled peoples
- **Ceremonial Robes**: Special clothing worn during important religious observances

#### **Metalwork & Jewelry**

- **Memory Pieces**: Jewelry that incorporates symbols representing important life events
- **Protective Amulets**: Metalwork designed to provide real magical benefits
- **Trade Silver**: Distinctive decorative items that serve as both art and currency
- **Ceremonial Objects**: Religious artifacts used in divination and ritual practices

## Language & Communication

### The Wandering Tongue (*Ibaialdeko Hizkuntza*)

#### **Linguistic Characteristics**

- **Agglutinative Structure**: Complex words built from multiple meaningful parts
- **Temporal Emphasis**: Elaborate system for expressing time relationships and sequences
- **Movement Vocabulary**: Rich terminology for describing different types of travel and motion
- **Sacred Registers**: Special language forms used only in religious or mystical contexts

#### **Writing System**

- **Limited Literacy**: Most cultural knowledge preserved orally rather than in writing
- **Symbolic Notation**: Simple symbol system used for marking trade agreements and family records
- **Sacred Scripts**: Mystical writing used only by seers and for religious purposes
- **Imperial Adaptation**: Some Wandering People learn Imperial Common for trade purposes

### Communication Customs

#### **Storytelling Protocols**

- **Formal Introduction**: Traditional phrases used when beginning important narratives
- **Audience Participation**: Expected responses and interactions during storytelling
- **Sacred Silence**: Moments when listeners must remain completely quiet
- **Story Ownership**: Rules about who can tell which stories and under what circumstances

#### **Trade Language**

- **Commercial Pidgin**: Simplified communication system used with settled peoples
- **Numerical Systems**: Special counting methods used for complex business transactions
- **Quality Indicators**: Terminology for describing the value and condition of trade goods
- **Trust Markers**: Verbal and physical signs used to establish credibility in business

## Economics & Trade

### Traditional Economy

#### **Goods & Services**

- **Mystical Services**: Fortune telling, charm making, and supernatural problem solving
- **Artisan Crafts**: High-quality metalwork, textiles, and decorative objects
- **Animal Trading**: Horses, livestock, and exotic animals acquired during travels
- **Information Brokerage**: News, gossip, and intelligence gathered from across the continent

#### **Trading Practices**

- **Seasonal Markets**: Participation in established trade fairs and market gatherings
- **Barter Systems**: Complex exchange networks that minimize dependence on imperial currency
- **Credit Networks**: Trust-based arrangements that allow for delayed payment and long-term agreements
- **Quality Reputation**: Business success based on maintaining excellent reputation across generations

### Relationship with Settled Economy

#### **Imperial Integration**

- **Tax Avoidance**: Nomadic lifestyle allows them to minimize imperial taxation
- **Legal Ambiguity**: Uncertain status under imperial law creates both opportunities and vulnerabilities
- **Cultural Exchange**: Introduction of new ideas and products through trade relationships
- **Economic Niche**: Filling market gaps that settled merchants cannot efficiently address

#### **Local Relationships**

- **Seasonal Partnerships**: Regular business relationships with specific towns and regions
- **Service Monopolies**: Unique skills that give them competitive advantages in certain markets
- **Cultural Barriers**: Prejudice and misunderstanding that can limit economic opportunities
- **Mutual Dependence**: Recognition that both nomadic and settled peoples benefit from trade

## Social Structure & Family Life

### Family Organization

#### **Extended Kinship Networks**

- **Multi-Generational Units**: Families including grandparents, parents, children, and related individuals
- **Adoption Practices**: Integration of outsiders into family units through formal ceremonies
- **Marriage Alliances**: Strategic partnerships between families that strengthen cultural bonds
- **Inheritance Patterns**: Complex systems for passing down both material and cultural wealth

#### **Gender Roles**

- **Complementary Partnerships**: Men and women have different but equally valued roles
- **Skill Specialization**: Certain crafts and abilities traditionally associated with specific genders
- **Leadership Flexibility**: Both men and women can hold positions of authority based on ability
- **Sacred Roles**: Some mystical abilities and religious positions are gender-specific

### Child-Rearing & Education

#### **Traditional Learning**

- **Oral Education**: Children learn through listening to stories, songs, and conversations
- **Practical Training**: Direct participation in family trades and daily survival activities
- **Cultural Immersion**: Constant exposure to traditional values and practices
- **Mentorship Systems**: Pairing young people with elders for specialized instruction

#### **Coming-of-Age Rituals**

- **The First Journey**: Solo travel requirement for adolescents to prove their readiness for adulthood
- **Skill Demonstration**: Public display of mastery in chosen craft or mystical ability
- **Story Contribution**: Addition of new experience or knowledge to family oral tradition
- **Sacred Recognition**: Formal acknowledgment of adult status by family and community elders

## Relationships with Settled Peoples

### Cultural Tensions

#### **Common Prejudices**

- **Stereotyping**: Assumptions about criminality, untrustworthiness, and cultural inferiority
- **Religious Suspicion**: Fear of mystical abilities and non-imperial spiritual practices
- **Economic Resentment**: Jealousy over perceived advantages of nomadic lifestyle
- **Legal Discrimination**: Systematic disadvantages in imperial legal systems

#### **Mutual Misunderstandings**

- **Settlement vs. Movement**: Fundamental disagreement about proper way to live
- **Individual vs. Community**: Different perspectives on personal responsibility and group loyalty
- **Written vs. Oral**: Conflicts over record-keeping and the preservation of agreements
- **Secular vs. Sacred**: Disputes over the role of spirituality in daily life

### Positive Interactions

#### **Cultural Exchange**

- **Knowledge Sharing**: Wandering People bring news and information from distant places
- **Artistic Influence**: Introduction of new musical styles, decorative techniques, and storytelling forms
- **Medical Knowledge**: Traditional healing practices that complement imperial medicine
- **Spiritual Services**: Mystical abilities that address needs not met by imperial institutions

#### **Economic Benefits**

- **Trade Opportunities**: Access to goods and services not available through regular imperial commerce
- **Skill Transfer**: Teaching of specialized crafts and techniques to settled communities
- **Entertainment Value**: Music, dance, and storytelling that enriches local cultural life
- **Problem Solving**: Unique perspectives and abilities that help resolve difficult situations

## Modern Challenges & Adaptations

### Imperial Expansion

#### **Territorial Pressure**

- **Road Restrictions**: Imperial control making traditional travel routes more difficult
- **Settlement Expansion**: Growing towns and cities reducing available open space
- **Legal Constraints**: Imperial laws that conflict with traditional Wandering People practices
- **Cultural Assimilation**: Pressure to abandon nomadic lifestyle and adopt imperial customs

#### **Adaptive Strategies**

- **Documentation**: Some families obtaining imperial travel permits and identification
- **Diplomatic Relations**: Establishing formal relationships with imperial authorities
- **Legal Advocacy**: Working within imperial systems to protect traditional rights
- **Cultural Preservation**: Intensified efforts to maintain traditions under external pressure

### Technological Changes

#### **Transportation Evolution**

- **Improved Roads**: Imperial road-building affecting traditional travel patterns
- **New Vehicles**: Adoption of imperial wagon designs and transportation technology
- **Communication Networks**: Imperial postal systems providing alternatives to traditional information sharing
- **Navigation Tools**: Integration of imperial maps and navigation instruments

#### **Economic Adaptation**

- **Imperial Currency**: Increased use of imperial coinage in trade relationships
- **Market Integration**: Participation in imperial commercial systems and trade guilds
- **Product Innovation**: Development of new goods designed for imperial markets
- **Service Expansion**: Offering traditional skills in new contexts and applications

## Notable Traditions & Ceremonies

### Life Cycle Rituals

#### **Birth Ceremonies**

- **Path Blessing**: Ritual to determine the spiritual significance of a child's birth time and location
- **Name Weaving**: Complex ceremony for choosing names that reflect family history and future potential
- **First Journey**: Symbolic travel undertaken shortly after birth to introduce children to the road
- **Ancestor Introduction**: Presentation of newborns to family spirits and cultural guardians

#### **Coming of Age**

- **The Solo Walk**: Required journey that adolescents must complete alone to prove readiness for adulthood
- **Skill Mastery**: Demonstration of competence in chosen craft, trade, or mystical ability
- **Story Addition**: Contribution of new knowledge or experience to family oral tradition
- **Community Recognition**: Formal acceptance as adult member of the extended family unit

#### **Marriage Customs**

- **Path Joining**: Ceremony that symbolically merges the travel routes of two family lines
- **Gift Exchange**: Complex system of presents that establish economic relationships between families
- **Story Binding**: Ritual that weaves together the oral histories of two family groups
- **Future Reading**: Divination ceremony to predict the success and challenges of the new union

#### **Death Observances**

- **Final Journey**: Ritual travel that honors the deceased and helps their spirit find its proper path
- **Memory Weaving**: Ceremony for preserving the stories and wisdom of the departed
- **Possession Distribution**: Formal process for distributing belongings according to traditional rules
- **Ancestor Elevation**: Recognition of the deceased as family guardian and spiritual guide

### Seasonal Celebrations

#### **Spring Awakening (*Udaberri-Esnatze*)**

- **Road Opening**: Ceremony marking the beginning of active travel season
- **Blessing of Wagons**: Ritual preparation of vehicles and equipment for the coming year
- **Marriage Season**: Time when most weddings take place among the Wandering People
- **Trade Planning**: Community meetings to coordinate commercial activities and route planning

#### **Summer Convergence (*Udako Bilkura*)**

- **Family Gathering**: Large assemblies where multiple family groups meet for various purposes
- **Storytelling Competition**: Formal contests to determine the best storytellers and preserve narratives
- **Craft Exhibition**: Display and sale of finest artisan work produced during the year
- **Youth Initiation**: Coming-of-age ceremonies for adolescents ready to enter adulthood

#### **Autumn Council (*Udazken-Kontseilua*)**

- **Route Planning**: Community decisions about winter travel patterns and destinations
- **Dispute Resolution**: Formal hearings for settling conflicts between families or individuals
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Exchange of new information, skills, and mystical discoveries
- **Winter Preparation**: Practical and spiritual preparations for the challenging cold season

#### **Winter Reflection (*Neguko Gogoetak*)**

- **Ancestor Veneration**: Extended ceremonies honoring deceased family members and cultural heroes
- **Story Marathons**: Long evenings devoted to telling and retelling traditional narratives
- **Divination Focus**: Intensive fortune-telling and mystical practices to predict the coming year
- **Craft Intensification**: Period of concentrated work on artistic and practical projects

### Sacred Observances

#### **The Great Gathering (*Bilkura Nagusia*)**

- **Generational Event**: Massive assembly of Wandering People that occurs only once per generation
- **Cultural Renewal**: Opportunity to update traditions and address major cultural challenges
- **Leadership Selection**: Choice of overall cultural leaders and spiritual guides
- **Sacred Storytelling**: Performance of the most important and secret cultural narratives

#### **Eclipse Rituals (*Eklipse-Errituak*)**

- **Cosmic Significance**: Solar and lunar eclipses are considered times of great spiritual power
- **Prophecy Reception**: Enhanced divination abilities during astronomical events
- **Protective Measures**: Ritual work to defend against spiritual dangers associated with eclipses
- **Cultural Meditation**: Community reflection on the meaning and direction of Wandering People culture

#### **Sacred Site Pilgrimages (*Leku Sakratuen Erromesealdiak*)**

- **Traditional Routes**: Journeys to ancient locations significant to Wandering People spirituality
- **Healing Ceremonies**: Ritual work performed at sites believed to have curative properties
- **Vision Quests**: Mystical practices undertaken at locations where the spiritual world is more accessible
- **Cultural Preservation**: Maintenance and protection of sacred sites from damage or desecration
